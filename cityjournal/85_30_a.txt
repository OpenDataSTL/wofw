Resolution No. 180
WHEREAS, the St. Louis City Board
of Aldermen is committed to efforts and
supports initiatives that will enhance the
quality of life of all citizens of Missouri; and
WHEREAS, programs to fund hospital
trauma care and emergency preparedness;
health care treatment and access; prescription
drug assistance for seniors; health care
initiatives for low income citizens, women,
minorities and children; tobacco prevention
and cessation programs; and research to help
find the cures to some of the most devastating
diseases afflicting our citizens will help make
Missouri a healthier place for all; and
WHEREAS, thousands of Missourians
die each year from trauma and Missouri’s
trauma and emergency preparedness systems
are terribly under-resourced and overburdened; and
WHEREAS, the cost of prescription
drugs is escalating out of control and Missouri
seniors need relief; and

WHEREAS, childhood care and
education programs can ensure that Missouri
children have healthy minds and bodies, and
start school ready to learn; and
WHEREAS, smoking prevention and
cessation programs to reach our children
before they start will ensure that more
Missourians choose a healthy, tobacco free
lifestyle; and
WHEREAS, studies show aggressive
tobacco prevention campaigns are effective
and do deter children from taking up the
deadly habit. But, Missouri ranks dead last in
the nation when it comes to investing in
tobacco prevention efforts; and
WHEREAS, Missouri is an evolving
leader in research to find treatment and cures
for our deadliest disease, bu the effort needs
support; and
WHEREAS, a 55 cent increase in the
excise tax on a pack of cigarettes, and an
additional tax of 20 percent of the
manufacturer’s invoice for other tobacco
products would generate net annual revenues
of approximately $342,636,000; and
WHEREAS,
Missourians
overwhelmingly support raising the tobacco
tax to improve the health and health care in
Missouri, as evidenced by the nearly 130,000
Missourians who signed the petition to place
this initiative on the ballot;
NOW THEREFORE BE IT
RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of the
City of St. Louis that we support the Healthy
Missouri ballot initiative to amend the
Missouri law to impose an additional excise
tax of 55 cents per pack–and 20 percent on
other tobacco products to be placed in the
Healthy Families Trust Fund to be used for
the following purposes: hospital trauma care
and emergency preparedness; health care
treatment and access; prescription drug
assistance for seniors; health care initiatives
for low income citizens, women, minorities
and children; life sciences research, including
medical research and the proper administration
of funds for such research; tobacco prevention
and cessation programs and grants for early
childhood care and education;
AND BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED
that this new law will help make Missouri a
healthier place by improving the health of our
children, our seniors and our families.
