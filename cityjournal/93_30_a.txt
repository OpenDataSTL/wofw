Resolution No. 196

Jamison Memorial Christian
Methodist Church
WHEREAS, Jamison Memorial
Christian Methodist Church, Reverend
Marcus J. Rogers, Pastor, was founded 93
years ago in the City of St. Louis, Missouri
by Reverend Nathaniel Moore in 1917; and

WHEREAS, Jamison Memorial CME
Church has maintained its establishment in
varying locations within the City of St. Louis

and since October 22, 1945, is currently
situated at 609 North Leffingwell Avenue in
the City’s Nineteenth Ward; and
WHEREAS, Jamison Memorial CME
Church will celebrate its Ninety-Three years
of Christian service and fellowship on Sunday,
October 31, 2010; and
WHEREAS, during this Great
Celebration, Jamison CME Church will
acknowledge its historical roots in the
December 16, 1870 organization of the Colored
Methodist Episcopal Church establishing its
independence as a direct result of the transition
from slavery to freedom; and
WHEREAS, the rich legacy of the
Christian Methodist Episcopal Church
reflects the significance of the black church in
the cultural, educational, political, social and
spiritual growth of African Americans; and
WHEREAS, Pastor Rev. Marcus J.
Rogers calls upon all members of Jamison
Memorial, past and present as well as the St.
Louis Community to come, reunite, rejoice
and give praise for the continuation of this
most cherished legacy during JAMISON’S
HOMECOMING CELEBRATION.
NOW THEREFORE BE IT
RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of
the City of St. Louis that we pause in our
deliberations to recognize Jamison Memorial
Christian Methodist Church’s Ninety-Three
Years of fellowship, worship and service
within the City of St. Louis and we further
direct the Clerk of this Board to spread a copy
of this Resolution across the minutes of these
proceedings and to prepare a commemorative
copy to the end that it may be presented to
our honorees at a time and place deemed
appropriate by the Sponsor.
Introduced on the 8th day of October, 2010
by:
Honorable Marlene E. Davis,Alderman 19th Ward

Resolution No. 218

National Domestic Violence
Awareness Month
WHEREAS, domestic violence is the
leading cause of injury to women between the
ages of 15 to 45, more than mugging and auto
accidents combined, females ages 16-24 are
the group most at risk of abuse, and one in
four women will experience domestic violence
in her lifetime; and

WHEREAS, Missouri domestic
violence programs provided a safe place to
stay to more than 10,500 women, children
and men in 2009, but forced to turn away
more than 15,000 others due to lack of space
; and

WHEREAS, Missouri law enforcement
agencies recorded 36,943 domestic violence
incidents this past year; and
WHEREAS, all city residents must work
together to educate our population about what
can be done to prevent domestic violence and
sexual assault and increase support to
survivors, their significant others, and the
agencies that provide services to survivors;
and
WHEREAS, collaborative efforts such
as Every Step Counts Organization, the Walk
In Her Shoes program, Forest Park Center for
Family Resources, Steps That Count 5K Race/
Walk and the Awareness Is What’s Up
Collegiate Awareness Program are designed to
increase awareness and celebrate the lives of
survivors and the service organizations that
support them; and
WHEREAS, during the National
Domestic Violence Awareness Month and
Celebrate Survivors’ Week to be recognized
the 3rd week of October, state and federal
agencies, consumer groups, women’s
organization and colleges and universities will
provide public awareness activities and events
dedicated to awareness and the celebration of
survivors of domestic violence.
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT
RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of
the City of St. Louis that we pause in our
deliberations to encourage all city residents to
increase domestic violence awareness in our
schools and in our communities and to celebrate
the lives of survivors of domestic violence

Resolution No. 220
A Resolution in Support of the
expansion of the National Park
Service to Oversee and Operate
Grant’s Farm
WHEREAS, Grant’s Farm is an
extraordinary treasure for the entire St. Louis
region and is one the premier attractions for
visitors coming to St. Louis from across the
country and the world; and
WHEREAS, Anheuser-Busch and the
Busch Family are to be commended for
preserving, maintaining and operating Grant’s
Farm as a historical site and as a premier tourist
attraction in the region; and
WHEREAS, Grant’s Farm has served
for generations as the home of the August A.

Busch family, an emigrant family who came
to America and built a business dynasty that
contributed to the employment of thousands
of Americans for several generations and
whose generosity affected the lives of
Americans in countless ways; and

WHEREAS, the Board of Aldermen
hereby supports the addition of Grant’s Farm
as a unit of the National Park Service, and
urges the appropriate authorities to take the
necessary steps to ensure Grant’s Farm’s
preservation of this national treasure for future
generations; and

WHEREAS, the Board of Aldermen
urges the National Park Service to explore
partnership with other organizations that may
facilitate the transfer of Grant’s Farm to the
National Park Service; and

WHEREAS, the Board of Aldermen
firmly believes that it is in the best interest of
current residents, neighboring residents, future
generations that this land and these facilities
would best be preserved under the protection
of the National Park Service in order that they
remain, as they are today, a valued treasure
which provides learning experiences for
visitors throughout the region.

NOW THEREFORE BE IT
RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen for
the City of St. Louis that the Board of
Aldermen supports the addition of Grant’s
Farm as a unit of the National Park service,
and urges the appropriate authorities to take
necessary steps to ensure Grant’s farm’s
preservation in this manner and furthermore,
that copies of this resolution are to be delivered
to the Secretary of the Interior and to the
congressional delegation of the State of
Missouri to demonstrate the Board of
Aldermen for the City of St. Louis’ support
of this acquisition.
