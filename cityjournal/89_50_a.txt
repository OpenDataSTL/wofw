Board Bill No. 201
(Floor Substitute)

An ordinance pertaining to Secondhand
Dealers and Junk Dealers as codified in Section
8.82 of the Revised Code of the City of St.
Louis; repealing Ordinances 30179, 41746,
55783, 55790, 58160, and 63478 and enacting
in lieu thereof a new ordinance establishing
licensing requirements and rules and
regulations for persons doing business in the
City of St. Louis as Secondhand Dealers and
Junk Dealers; containing definitions; a penalty
clause and an emergency clause.

Board Bill No. 416
(Committee Substitute)

An ordinance recommended by the Board
of Public Service, authorizing and directing
the Mayor and Comptroller to enter into and
execute, on behalf of the City, a Wi-FI Access
Agreement (“Agreement”) between the City
of St. Louis (the “City”) and SBC Internet
Services, Inc. (a California corporation), d/b/a
AT&T Internet Services (“the Company”)
pursuant to which Company will access and
use City property, as defined in the
Agreement, to construct and operate a wireless
broadband internet access system for public
use and to provide free and fee wireless
broadband internet access, on terms and
conditions set forth in the Agreement, a form
of which is attached hereto, marked Exhibit 1
and incorporated herein by reference.

Board Bill No. 407

An ordinance designating a portion of
the City of St. Louis, Missouri generally
bounded by Eighth Street on the west, Walnut
Street on the north, South Broadway on the
east, and Clark Street on the south as a
Development Area (the “Development Area”)
under the authority of the Missouri
Downtown and Rural Economic Stimulus Act,
Sections 99.915 TO 99.1060 of the Revised
Statutes of Missouri, as amended (the “Act”);
approving a Development Plan for the
Development Area, and a Development

Project therein and making findings relating
thereto, adopting development financing;
establishing a special allocation fund;
authorizing certain actions by city officials;
and containing a severability clause.

Board Bill No. 409
(Committee Substitute)

Development Plan, Development Area, and
Development Project under the authority of
the Missouri Downtown and Rural Economic
Stimulus Act, Sections 99.915 TO 99.1060 of
the Revised Statutes of Missouri, as amended
(the “Act”); authorizing and directing the
execution of a Development Agreement
between the City of St. Louis and Ballpark
Village Holding Company, LLC in accordance
with the Act and said Development Plan;
prescribing the form and details of said
agreement; making certain findings as required
by the Act with respect to said agreement;
designating Ballpark Village Holding
Company, LLC as Developer of the
Development Area in accordance with the Act;
making certain findings with respect thereto;
authorizing other related actions by city
officials in connection with the agreement and
the development of certain property within
the Development Area; and containing a
severability clause.

(Committee Substitute)

Resolution No. 208

WHEREAS, The St. Louis Board of
Aldermen has a long standing tradition of
supporting and honoring the members of the
St. Louis Metropolitan Police Department
who place themselves at substantial risk to
protect and serve the residents of this
community; and

WHEREAS, one of these outstanding
officers is Police Officer Michael Minor who
is a two year veteran of the St. Louis Police
Department and currently attends Webster
University where he is pursuing a degree in
Business Management; and

WHEREAS, Office Minor is assigned
to the Second District; and

WHEREAS, Officer Minor has been
chosen Officer of the Month in the Second
District three times during the past year in
recognition of his outstanding professionalism
and dedication to duty; and

WHEREAS, Officer Minor ’s
investigative skills, integrity and sincere
friendship have earned him the respect and
admiration of his fellow officers and coworkers; and

WHEREAS, as a result of his
outstanding effort, Officer Minor has been
recognized as the 2006 Officer of the Year for
the Second District by the St. Louis
Metropolitan Police Department;

NOW THEREFORE BE IT
RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of
the City of St. Louis that we pause in our
deliberations to recognize and commend Police
Officer Michael Minor for his dedication to
duty and we thank him, on behalf of the
citizens of the City of St. Louis, for his service
to this community and we direct the Clerk of

this Board to spread a copy of this Resolution
across the minutes of these proceedings and
to further prepare a commemorative copy to
the end that it may be presented to our honoree
at a time and place deemed appropriate by
the Sponsor.
Introduced on the 9th day of February, 2007
by:
Honorable Donna Baringer,Alderwoman 16th Ward

Resolution No. 209

WHEREAS, Myrtle French was born
to James and Mary Long on August 31, 1927,
and was living in St. Louis, Missouri on
February 8, 2007 when God took her home to
the love and beauty of His everlasting light;
and

WHEREAS, Myrtle French will be
deeply missed by son Fernandel French;
grandchildren Antonio D. French, Diane F.
Donaldson, and Deon F. Donaldson; sister
Mildred Phipps and her husband, Robert, and
children; sister Barbara Gess and her husband,
Col. William Gess, and children and
grandchildren; niece Shirley Spencer and
children and grandchildren; nephew Jimmy
Williams and children; and countless other
family members, neighbors, and friends; and

WHEREAS, Myrtle French began her
life in politics alongside then-husband, R.C.
French, as an organizer in the 1960s in the old
20th Ward working with Committeeman Leroy
Tyus and Committeewoman Geneva Wright;
and

WHEREAS, in the 1970s, Myrtle
French moved into the 21st Ward and became
a close ally of State Representative Russell
Goward and Committeewoman Jackie Butler,
and was first elected Committeewoman in
1996, a position she continued to hold at the
time of her passing; and
WHEREAS, Myrtle French diligently
served the City of St. Louis as a Deputy Sheriff
from November 1983 to March 1994; and
WHEREAS, Myrtle French now joins
her beloved daughter, Diane French
Donaldson; sister Marie O. Williams; mother
Mary Lee Long; and father James Long, Jr.;
and
WHEREAS, Myrtle French will be
remembered for her strength, charm,
determination, wisdom, laughter, and as a
public servant of the highest caliber; and
NOW THEREFORE BE IT
RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of
the City of St. Louis that we pause in our
deliberations to remember the life and service
of Myrtle French and we join with her many
friends in expressing our sorrow at her passing

and we direct the Clerk of this Board to
prepare a memorial copy of this Resolution
to the end that it may be presented the French
family at a time and place deemed appropriate
by the Sponsor.
Introduced on the 14th day of February, 2007
by:
Honorable Bennice Jones King,Alderwoman 21stWard

Resolution No. 210
WHEREAS, the legendary St. Louis
Argus newspaper has been providing
invaluable service to the St. Louis Community
for over 95 years; and
WHEREAS, the St. Louis Argus
newspaper stands as the oldest continuous
black owned business in Missouri; and
WHEREAS, the St. Louis Argus has
continuously addressed critical civic and
community issues present in the St. Louis
community; and
WHEREAS, the St. Louis Argus has as
part of its mission continues to identify and
support African-American leaders in their
quests to uplift the community; and
WHEREAS, the St. Louis Argus has also
provided critical public information and
advertising coverage for the many service
agencies in the community that provide critical
programming for low to moderate income
families; and
WHEREAS, the St. Louis Argus has
once again distinguished itself by celebrating
the “Lunch with the Legends” in celebration
of Black History Month and in recognition of
African American leaders in St. Louis who
have achieved the status of being the “first” in
their fields; and
WHEREAS, the St. Louis Argus will
host this historic event on Saturday, February
24, 2007 at the Sheraton St. Louis City Center
Hotel under the dynamic leadership of its
leader Mr. Eddie Hassan;
NOW THEREFORE BE IT
RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of
the City of St. Louis, that we pause in our
deliberations to recognize and honor the
ownership and staff of the St. Louis Argus
and we thank them for more than 95 years
journalistic leadership in this community and
we further direct the Clerk of this Board to
spread a copy of this Resolution across the
minutes of these proceedings and to prepare a
commemorative copy for presentation to our
honorees at a time and place deemed
appropriate by the Sponsor.
Introduced on the 24th day of February, 2007
by:

Honorable Lewis Reed, Alderman 6th Ward

Resolution No. 216

WHEREAS, we have been apprised of
the untimely passing of a prominent member
of our community and exceptional public
servant, John F. Bass on Sunday, February
25, 2007; and

WHEREAS, Mr. Bass was born and
raised in St. Louis and was a distinguished
graduate of Lincoln University and Washington
University; and

WHEREAS, after serving as both a
teacher and principal at Beaumont High School
in the late 1960’s, Mr. Bass began his
extraordinary career in public service in 1971
as the Director of the Welfare Department for
the City of St. Louis under then Mayor Al
Cervantes; and

WHEREAS, in 1973 Mr. Bass was
elected Comptroller for the City of St. Louis,
becoming the first African American to hold a
city-wide elected position and sit on the
influential Board of Estimate and
Apportionment; and

WHEREAS, Mr. Bass’s political career
spanned two decades during which time he
served as Alderman for the 20th Ward, Director
of the Missouri State Department of
Transportation, Missouri State Senator and
staff director for a Congressional
subcommittee in Washington D.C.; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Bass was an
inspirational champion for the City of St.
Louis and a indispensable role model and
mentor for a generation of African American
leaders in this community; and
WHEREAS, throughout his life of
service Mr. Bass enjoyed the love and support
of his late wife, Frieda, his daughter Jill, his
grandchildren and great grandchildren, his
devoted companion Marietta, his family and
his many friends and admirers who all cherish

his memory and his enduring legacy of
integrity, professionalism and public service;
NOW THEREFORE BE IT
RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of
the City of St. Louis, that we pause in our
deliberations to recall the outstanding service
and commitment of John F. Bass to the citizens
of the City of St. Louis and we join with his
countless friends and admirers in expressing
our sorrow at his passing and we further direct
the Clerk of this Board to spread a copy of
this Resolution across the minutes of these
proceedings and to prepare a commemorative
copy for presentation to the Bass family at a
time and place deemed appropriate by the
Sponsor.
