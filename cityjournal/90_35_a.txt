Resolution No. 236

WHEREAS, we have been apprised that
Eugene B. Redmond will be celebrating an
auspicious career as an educator, a writer, and
a poet; and

WHEREAS, Mr. Redmond will be
celebrating retirement as Professor of English,
African American Literature andCreative
Writing at Southern Illinois UniversityEdwardsville; his 70th Birthday (December
1st); release of Drumvoices Revue (#15) &
Images & Homages: ‘Memoirs’ from the EBR
Collection, co-published by the Eugene B.
Redmond Writers Club (chartered in 1986 by
Sherman Fowler, Darlene Roy & Mr.
Redmond) and SIUE; and

WHEREAS, Mr. Redmond has the
distinction of being Poet Laureate of East Saint

Louis since 1976 and the Founding Editor of
Drumvoices Revue. During the early-mid
1960’s, he edited SIUE’s Alestle & the East
St. Louis Monitor under the late Clyde C.
Jordan. He “souljourned” to The March on
Washington. He published poems in St.
Louis-area journals—Perspectives, Free Lance
& Tambourine. He was cited by the Chicago
Tribune as one of 25 outstanding college
students in America. He won poetry awards
sponsored by Washington University’s
Annual Fine Arts Festival & Free Lance
magazine. He wrote proposals for the Human
Development Corporation; and
WHEREAS, during the mid-late 1960’s,
he became a leader in Civil Rights/Black Power
movements & regional/national literary circles.
He was Director of Head Start at Grace Hill
Settlement House.
His other
accomplishments included Teacher-Counselor
& Poet-in-Residence at SIU-East Louis’
Experiment in Higher Education; a teacher with
Henry Dumas, Oliver Jackson, & Katherine
Dunham at the Performing Arts Training
Center; anthologized in The New Black Poetry
and Nigeria’s Black Orpheus; a performer with
Black Artists Group (BAG) in Laclede Town
& Lafayette Park; a teacher in Webster
University’s Upward Bound Program &
Writer-in-Residence at Oberlin College (Ohio);
and
WHEREAS, from 1970-85, Mr.
Redmond was Professor of English and Poetin-Residence in Ethnic Studies at California
State University-Sacramento; won an
Outstanding Faculty Research Award & a
National Endowment for the Arts Creative
Writing Fellowship; published Drumvoices:
The Mission of Afro-American Poetry plus
Sentry of the Four Golden Pillars, River of
Bones and Flesh and Blood, & Songs from an
Afro-Phone; edited Sides of the River & Griefs
of Joy: Anthology of Afro-American Poetry
for Students, recorded an LP/CD, Bloodlinks
& Sacred Places, and became Visiting
Professor at University of WisconsinMadison; and
WHEREAS, in 1990, he rejoined the
faculty of SIUE; in 1998, he was invited by
Oprah Winfrey to read an original poem at
Maya Angelou’s 70th birthday gala; in 1999,
he joined Maya Angelou, Toni Morrison,
Walter Mosley, Lerone Bennett Jr., August
Wilson & Henry Dumas as an inductee into
the International Hall of Fame for Writers of
African Descent; in 2003, received Staying
the Course Award with Angela Bassett and
Gordon Parks in Chicago; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Redmond experienced
the fruits of his photographic labors in 2004
when “Visualizing Black Writers: An ExtraLiterary Exhibit from the ugene B. Redmond

Collection” received international premieres
at SIUE’s Morris University Center Gallery
& the African Studies Institute Gallery at the
University of Ibadan in Nigeria; and

WHEREAS, Mr. Redmond has authored
or edited dozens of collections of poetry &
diverse writings, including plays for stage and
TV, and the posthumously published works
of Dumas; received a Lifetime Achievement
Award from Pan-African Movement USA, a
National Endowment for the Humanities
Grant, a Pushcart Prize: Best of the Small
Presses, a Tribute to Elder Honor from the
African Poetry Theater of NYC, an American
Book Award for The Eye in the Ceiling, and
released Blues-Ode to the ForeDreamers &
Other Poems in 2001; and

WHEREAS, this year, he was inducted
into the Senior Illinoisans Hall of Fame at the
Governor’s Mansion in Springfield and
received the Sterling Brown Award for Lifetime
Achievement from the African American
Literature and Culture Society of the American
Literature Association during AALCS’s
Symposium at St. Louis University.

NOW THERFORE BE IT
RESOLVED by the Board of Alderman of
the City of St. Louis, that we pause in our
deliberations to congratulate and recognize
Eugene B. Redmond for his lifetime of
accomplishments as an educator, a writer and
a poet; and we wish him peace and happiness
in his retirement, and we direct the Clerk of
this Board to spread a copy of this Resolution
across the minutes of these proceedings and
to further prepare a commemorative copy to
the end that it may be presented to our honoree
at a time and place deemed appropriate by
the Sponsor.

Introduced on the 16th day of November, 2007
Honorable Charles QuincyTroupe,Alderman 1st Ward
