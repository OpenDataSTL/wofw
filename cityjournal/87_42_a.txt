Resolution No. 207

WHEREAS, we have been apprised that
after more than 21 years of dedicated service
to the Danforth Foundation, Bruce J.
Anderson has announced his retirement; and

WHEREAS, Dr. Anderson is a native of
Thomaston, Connecticut and is a distinguished
graduate of Central Connecticut State
University and the University of Virginia; and

WHEREAS, prior to joining the staff at
the Danforth Foundation as vice-president
responsible for grants and programs in

precollegiate education, Dr. Anderson served
as a highly respected educator at universities
in Connecticut and Virginia and was a
nationally recognized advocate for university
based leadership preparation programs; and
WHEREAS, since 1991, Dr. Anderson
has served as President of the Danforth
Foundation and has worked closely with the
Danforth family and the Foundation’s Board
of Trustees in their outstanding effort to
improve the quality of life for citizens
throughout the St. Louis region; and
WHEREAS, during his tenure as
President, Dr. Anderson has overseen the
awarding of more than $415 Million in grants
and programs designed to stabilize and
revitalize the St. Louis community; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Anderson is an
exceptional member of our community whose
service and dedication to our citizens is greatly
appreciated; and
WHEREAS, we are certain that Dr.
Anderson is looking forward to a well deserved
retirement with wife, Ann; his children, Chris,
Carrie and Mark and his many friends;
NOW THEREFORE BE IT
RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of
the City of St. Louis that we pause in our
deliberations to congratulate Bruce J.
Anderson for 21 years of service with the
Danforth Foundation and we wish him peace
and happiness in his retirement and we direct
the Clerk of this Board to spread a copy of
this Resolution across the minutes of these
proceedings and to further prepare a
commemorative copy to the end that it may
be presented to our honoree at a time and
place deemed appropriate by the Sponsor.
Introduced on the 31st day of December 2004
by:
Honorable Phyllis Young, Alderwoman 7th Ward

deliberations to note the untimely passing of
