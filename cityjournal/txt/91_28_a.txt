following board bills that were truly agreed to
and finally adopted.

Board Bill No. 115

An ordinance pertaining to street
vending within the Civic Center Vending
District; amending Section Four of Ordinance
65061 to designate the boundaries of the Civic
Center Vending District; establishing rules and
regulations for Civic Center Vending District
and containing an emergency clause.

Board Bill No. 137

An Ordinance pertaining to the Health
Insurance Portability and Accountability Act
of 1996, P.L. 104-91 (hereinafter HIPAA);
repealing Ordinance 66281 and enacting in lieu
thereof a new ordinance providing for
compliance by the City with both the HIPAA
Standards for Privacy of Individually
Identifiable Health Information, 45 C.F.R. Part
160 and Part 164, Subparts A and E (the
Privacy Rule) and the HIPAA Security
Standards for the Protection of Electronic
Protected Health Information, 45 C.F.R. Part
160 and Part 164, Subparts A and C (the
Security Rule); re-designating the City’s
Hybrid Entity Health Care Components, with
removal of the Health Department from such
designation; re-designating the City’s Business
Associate Components, with removal of the
Department of Public Service Building
Division, and requiring Business Associate
Components to meet certain requirements of
the Privacy Rule and Security Rule;
appointing the City Privacy Officer and the
Privacy Officers for each of the designated
Health Care Components, providing for their
duties, and providing for amendments of such
designations; appointing the City Security
Officer and the Security Officers for each of
the designated Health Care Components,
providing for their duties, and providing for
amendments of such designations; authorizing
the Health Care Component Privacy Officers,
upon recommendation of the City Counselor,
to enter into Business Associate Agreements
in compliance with the Privacy Rule and the
Security Rule, and upon recommendation of
the City Counselor and the Board of Estimate
and Apportionment, to enter into all other
agreements required for compliance with the
Privacy Rule and the Security Rule; and
providing for a severability clause.
David W. Sweeney, Clerk

Resolution No. 165
Ella Dell Cofer
WHEREAS, we pause in our
deliberations to note the untimely passing of
longtime St. Louis resident, Ella Dell Cofer;
and
WHEREAS, Ella Dell Cofer was born
April 30, 1917 in Starkville, Mississippi. She
was the daughter of Ezell Owens and Lou
Hines. Ella Dell was the youngest of 10
children. She accepted Christ at an early age.
She moved to St. Louis in 1935 to care for her
nephew, Robert Wallace. She joined the
Northern Baptist Church upon moving to St.
Louis; and
WHEREAS, on April 4, 1937 she
married Frank Cofer. To this union 3 children
were born, Carlinda (Cofer) McDaniels,
Dwight Cofer and Curtis Cofer; and
WHEREAS, Ella Dell loved to work on
the farm in Troy, Missouri. It was in Troy
that she began to study Jehovah with Mrs.
Joyce Kemp. She was later baptized and joined
the Newstead Congregation in St. Louis; and
WHEREAS, Ella Dell lived at 4415 St.
Ferdinand Avenue for over 65 years. She was
well known in her neighborhood for her good
advice and good cooking. Ella Dell nurtured,
raised and mentored many, many children and
adults in her lifetime. Many people loved her
and will sadly miss her good conversation,
advice, great cakes, pie, homemade rolls, ice
cream and much more; and
WHEREAS, she was active in her
community and a proud property owner. She
worked with many neighborhood
organizations to ensure the upkeep and safety
of the Ville area. She was a part of many
meetings that planned new ways of making
sure the historic greater Ville area was well
taken care of by residents and visitors; and

WHEREAS, on Wednesday, September
17, 2008 Ella Dell transitioned from a life of
pain and sickness to a better place with the
Lord; and
WHEREAS, she leaves to mourn her
passing one sister, Rena Monroe of Starkville,
Mississippi, 3 children, 5 grandchildren,
Michelle McDaniels, Kelly Cofer, Anthony
McDaniels, Anthony Tobias, and Carnita
McDaniels, one great-grandson, Brandon
Alexander, a host of nieces, nephews, cousins,
friends and godchildren.
NOW THEREFORE BE IT
RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of
the City of St. Louis that we pause to
remember the many contributions of Ella Dell
Cofer to the citizens of the City of St. Louis
and we join with her many friends in
expressing our sorrow at her passing, and we
further direct the Clerk of this Board to spread
a copy of this Resolution across the minutes
of these proceedings and to prepare a memorial
copy for presentation to the Cofer family, at
a time and place deemed appropriate by the
Sponsor.
Introduced on the 19th day of September, 2008
by:
Honorable Samuel L. MooreAlderman 4th Ward

Government; and

WHEREAS, prior to his legislative

Kit Bond, John Ashcroft, and Jim Talent; and

represented part of St. Charles County

Representatives. He was first elected to the

in 2004 with 73% of the vote. During the
92nd Missouri General Assembly,

Majority Whip and served as Vice-Chairman
of the Financial Services Committee. He also
served on the Education Committee, and the
Small Business Committee; and
WHEREAS, during his first year as a

legislator, Missouri’s community college
chancellors and presidents, named Sherman

also named Missouri Votes Conservation’s
Rising Stars, due to his leadership on
conservation issues; and
WHEREAS, in 2002, Sherman was
named by his peers as the Missouri’s Young
Republican of the Year. In 2005, Cardinal
Glennon Hospital awarded Sherman the
“Defender of the Children” award. From 2000
to 2007, he served on the Board of Directors
of the Boys and Girls Clubs of St. Charles
County. In 2006, Sherman was named by the
St. Louis Business Journal as one of the
region’s “Most Influential Minority Business
Leaders; and
WHEREAS, Sherman leaves to cherish
his memories, his wife, Julana Parker;
daughters, Alexandra & Jazmyn; Mother,

Resolution No. 168
6000-6100 Westminster 100th
Anniversary
WHEREAS, the first homes on 60006100 Westminster were built 100 years ago
beginning in 1908 following the 1904 World’s
Fair; and
WHEREAS, all 103 original Arts &
Crafts style homes are valuable and intact
having weathered everything the last 100
years had to offer; and
WHEREAS, 6000-6100 Westminster
have left their mark on the City as a Mayor,
aldermen (2); a state representative; school
board members (2) and other officials and
activists have called Westminster home; and
WHEREAS, neighbors of different
faiths, cultures, races, ages and economic levels
have shared yards and pools and porches; joys,
sorrows and celebrations; good times and sad
times; and
WHEREAS, the exceptional camaraderie
and comfortable living conditions on
Westminster lead residents to stay around and
some 50% of the neighbors have lived in their
home for 15 years or more; and
WHEREAS, Westminster neighbors
have worked together over the past 100 years
to fight crime and blight; improve safety and
appearance and have created a strong, vibrant
community of people who care about each
other and our great City; and
WHEREAS, Westminster residents look
forward to the next 100 years, embracing the
changes that will make St. Louis and its
neighborhoods grow and thrive while
cherishing the people and history that have
made us what we are today.

RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of
the City of St. Louis, that we wish to recognize
6000-6100 Westminster on the occasion of
their One Hundredth Anniversary, that we
encourage them to continue in their
commitment to the community, and we wish
it many more years of success; and we further
direct the Clerk of this Board to spread a copy
of this Resolution across the minutes of this
proceeding and to prepare a commemorative
copy for presentation to our honorees at a
time and place deemed appropriate by the
sponsor.

PUBLIC NOTICE
CITY OF ST. LOUIS
BOARD OF PUBLIC SERVICE
CONTRACTOR QUALIFICATIONS

The City of St. Louis, Board of Public
Service, will be requesting bids for a Roof
Replacement on the St. Louis Metropolitan
Police Academy building, 315 S. Tucker Blvd.,
St. Louis, MO. The roofing system will be a
Thermoplastic Polyolefin (TPO) roofing
system with a 20 year guarantee. The roof
area is roughly 8,500 S.F.

This project will require the contractors
to pay prevailing wages and will require
Payment and Performance bonds in the full

amount of the contract. The City also has
M/WBE goals of 25% and 5% for this project.
Contractors interested in bidding on the
project should complete the Contractor
Qualifications form which is available for
download from the Board of Public Service
