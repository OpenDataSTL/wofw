Resolution No. 199
Bishop Samuel White
WHEREAS, Bishop Samuel White has
served Christendom within the Church of God
in Christ for more than thirty-six years.
During this time, he served as an Ordained
Elder, a Junior Pastor, District
Superintendent, and Chairman of the Board
of Superintendents. He also served as
Jurisdictional Comptroller, Chairman of the
Budget Committee, Chairman of Finance,
Souvenir Booklet Chairman and an organizer
of Church Systems and Functions. He has
also served as a member of the Church of God
in Christ’s National Constitution Committee;
and
WHEREAS, Bishop White wrote the
Financial Plan for Districts and Jurisdictions,
which was adopted by the national church.
He was a contributor of information written
in the Jurisdictional Policy Procedural Manual,
which was adopted by the General Board and
the General Assembly of the Church of God
in Christ. He was a contributor of information
written in the book, “First Five Centuries of
the Christian Church,” by Dr. Donald Weeks;
and
WHEREAS, Bishop White is the author
of several books and pamphlets. He is a
workshop facilitator, a financial consultant and
the author of “Prudent Living in the 21st
Century.” He is the developer of widows of
deceased clergymen and deceased community

leaders across the states of Illinois, Arkansas,
Missouri and Tennessee; and
WHEREAS, Bishop White has served
on many distinguished boards and committees.
He served as the chairman for mentoring and
tutoring the next generation of leaders; and
WHEREAS, for exemplary service,
Bishop White has received many plaques and
certificates too numerous to list. He is
affiliated with many distinguished community
organizations; and
WHEREAS, of all the accolades that
could be given, Bishop White is most proud
to called, ‘Samuel, the Eternal God’s Son.’ He
is the Pastor of Monroe Church and Heavenly
Places Worldwide Ministries; and
WHEREAS, in July 2009, Bishop
White was chosen to serve as Bishop of the
Colleague of Bishops-the leaders of United
Fellowship International Ministries, Inc.
NOW WHEREFORE BE IT
RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of
the City of St. Louis that we pause in our
deliberations to recognize Bishop Samuel
White and we wish him continued success,
achievement and accomplishment and we
further direct the Clerk of this Board to spread
a copy of this Resolution across the minutes
of these proceedings and to prepare a
commemorative copy to the end that it may
be presented to our honoree at a time and
place deemed appropriate by the Sponsor.
Introduced on the 13th day of November, 2009
by:
Honorable Samuel L. Moore,Alderman 4th Ward

Resolution No. 200
America Recycles Day 2009
WHEREAS, the City of St. Louis
landfills approximately 200,000 tons of waste
each year, at the cost of about $6 million in
landfill fees annually; and
WHEREAS, a 2006-2007 Missouri
Municipal Solid Waste Composition study
revealed that a minimum of 38% of our City’s
waste is readily recyclable through our existing
markets; and
