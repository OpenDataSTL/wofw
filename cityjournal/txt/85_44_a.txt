Board Bill No. 313
(Committe Substitute)
An
ordinance
approving
a
Redevelopment Plan for the 4360-72 Lindell
Boulevard Area (“Area”) after finding that the
Area is blighted as defined in Section 99.320
of the Revised Statutes of Missouri, 1994, as
amended, (the “Statute” being Sections 99.300
to 99.715 inclusive), containing a description
of the boundaries of said Area in the City of
St. Louis (“City”), attached hereto and
incorporated herein as Exhibit “A”, finding
that redevelopment and rehabilitation of the
Area is in the interest of the public health,
safety, morals and general welfare of the people
of the City; approving the Plan dated October
22, 2002 for the Area (“Plan”), incorporated
herein by attached Exhibit “B”, pursuant to
Section 99.430; finding that there is a feasible
financial plan for the development of the Area
which affords maximum opportunity for
development of the Area by private enterprise;
finding that property in the Area may not be
acquired by the Land Clearance for
Redevelopment Authority of the City of St.
Louis (“LCRA”) through the existence of
eminent domain; finding that the property
withintheAreaisunoccupied, but if it
should become occupied the Redeveloper
shall be responsible for relocating any eligible
occupants displaced as a result of
implementation of the Plan; finding that there
shall be available ten (10) year real estate tax
abatement; and pledging cooperation of the
Board of Aldermen and requesting various
officials, departments, boards and agencies of
the city to cooperate and to exercise their
respective powers in a manner consistent with
the Plan.

Resolution No. 260
WHEREAS , Dutchtown South
Community Corporation is celebrating its 30th
anniversary in March 2003, having been
founded in March 1973; and
WHEREAS , Dutchtown South
Community Corporation was organized to
encourage preservation, renovation and
renewal of area property with an eye toward
maintaining neighborhood stability; and
WHEREAS, since its inception,
residents and businesses within the
Dutchtown South community have spent
millions of investment dollars in property
upkeep, rehabilitation, and development; and
WHEREAS, Dutchtown South
Community Corporation is a Neighborhood
Housing Development Corporation with the
following boundaries: Mississippi River on
the east; Utah at Compton and Chippewa at
Grand on the north; Missouri Pacific Railroad
tracks on the west; and Eichelberger on the
south; with approximately twenty-nine
thousand residents; and
WHEREAS , Dutchtown South
Community Corporation has informed its

membership through publication of a
newsletter and/or newspaper since May 1973;
and
WHEREAS , since 1978 the offices of
Dutchtown South Community Corporation
have been at 4204 Virginia at Meramec and
eight apartments for low-income, elderly
housing were created in the same building in
1981; and

WHEREAS , Dutchtown South
Community Corporation serves as a one-stop
source for marketing, promotion, information
and referral about area rental and sale
property; presents annual neighborhood
improvement awards; conducts house tours;
supports Operation Brightside in Region 2;
works closely with the First and Third District
Police, Operation Safestreet, Neighborhood
Watch, and National Night Out; maintains a
list of individuals who offer yard services and
home repair; promotes weatherization;
distributes paint and grass seed; assists
residents with problems and provides
information concerning their property;
advocates preservation of major community
gems, such as Meramec, Froebel, and Monroe
Elementary Schools and Al Smith’s Feasting
Fox Restaurant; and helps people, particularly
seniors, who need access to services;
NOW THEREFORE BE IT
RESOLVED that we pause in our
deliberations to celebrate Dutchtown South
Community Corporation’s thirty years of
service to the community. We thank the many
volunteers, members, directors, officers and
employees over the years who have devoted
countless hours. We direct the Clerk of this
Board to spread a copy of this Resolution
across the minutes of these proceedings and
to prepare a commemorative copy to the end
that it may be presented to our honorees at a
time and place deemed appropriate by the
Sponsors.
