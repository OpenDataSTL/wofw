Board of Aldermen Report, June 17,
2005, St. Louis, Missouri.

To the President of the Board of
Aldermen:

I wish to report that on the 10th day of
June, 2005, I delivered the Office of the Mayor
of the City of St. Louis the following board
bills that were truly agreed to and finally
adopted.

Board Bill No. 50
An
ordinance
approving
a
Redevelopment Plan for the 1556 & 1560
Kraft Street & 1565 Fairmount Avenue Area
(“Area”) after finding that the Area is blighted
as defined in Section 99.320 of the Revised
Statutes of Missouri, 2000, as amended, (the
“Statute” being Sections 99.300 to 99.715
inclusive), containing a description of the
boundaries of said Area in the City of St. Louis
(“City”), attached hereto and incorporated
herein as Exhibit “A”, finding that
redevelopment and rehabilitation of the Area
is in the interest of the public health, safety,
morals and general welfare of the people of
the City; approving the Plan dated January
25, 2005 for the Area (“Plan”), incorporated
herein by attached Exhibit “B”, pursuant to
Section 99.430; finding that there is a feasible
financial plan for the development of the Area
which affords maximum opportunity for
development of the Area by private enterprise;
finding that no property in the Area may be
acquired by the Land Clearance for
Redevelopment Authority of the City of St.
Louis (“LCRA”) through the exercise of
eminent domain; finding that the property
within the Area is unoccupied, but if it should
become occupied the Redeveloper shall be
responsible for relocating any eligible
occupants displaced as a result of
implementation of the Plan; finding that
financial aid may be necessary to enable the
Area to be redeveloped in accordance with
the Plan; finding that there shall be available
ten (10) year real estate tax abatement; and
pledging cooperation of the Board of
Aldermen and requesting various officials,
departments, boards and agencies of the City
to cooperate and to exercise their respective
powers in a manner consistent with the Plan.

community with a great sense of humor and

exceptional product that brings joy to all; and

WHEREAS,, Marvin, assisted by his

decades;

deliberations to recognize Marvin E. Smiddy
on the occasion of the 50th Anniversary of his

thank him for his outstanding commitment to

the Clerk of this Board to spread a copy of

Resolution No. 80

WHEREAS,, since 1994 the City of St.
Louis has annually appropriated funds from
the Prop M Sales Tax for transfer to the BiState Development Agency (“Metro”) for the
purpose of funding the Agency’s public
transportation purposes; and

WHEREAS,, these funds are currently
used for the Metrolink expansion which is
seriously behind schedule and over budget;
and

WHEREAS,, for many months we have
been aware of the multitude of problems
associated with this expansion, which include
contractors failing to perform their duties and
spiraling costs for labor and materials due to
the many delays; and

WHEREAS,, the funds used to make
this project possible come from the citizens
of this city in the form of a sales tax which
must be appropriated annually by this Board
of Aldermen; and

WHEREAS,, this Board has a duty to
hold Metro accountable for the expenditure
of public dollars and to insure that these funds
are used in a manner that will benefit the entire
community; and

WHEREAS,, as the governing body of
the City of St. Louis it is incumbent on this
Board to investigate the manner in which tax
dollars are spent and to open to public scrutiny
the policies and procedures employed by
Metro officials in authorizing the Metrolink
expenditures;

NOW THEREFORE BE IT
RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of
the City of St. Louis that we direct the that
the appointment of a special committee of
aldermen be considered to investigate Metro
and it’s policies regarding hiring, the awarding
of contracts and purchasing of supplies and
materials for the Metrolink expansion and to
further investigate the current delays and cost
overruns associated with this project and to

inquire of Metro officials as to the status of
future expansion within the City of St. Louis,
specifically expansion into North St. Louis
City;
We further direct the special committee
to report its findings to the full Board of
Aldermen within six months of the adoption
of this Resolution.

Resolution No. 82

WHEREAS,, we pause in our
deliberations to recognize the historical
significance of Juneteenth Independence Day,
and expressing the belief that history should
be regarded as a means for understanding the
past and solving the challenges of the future;
and

WHEREAS, news of the end of slavery
did not reach frontier areas of the United
States, and in particular the Southwestern
States, for more than 2 years after President
Lincoln’s Emancipation Proclamation of
January 1, 1863, and months after the
conclusion of the Civil War; and

WHEREAS, on June 19, 1865, Union
soldiers led by Major General Gordon Granger
arrived in Galveston, Texas, with news that
the Civil War had ended and that the enslaved
were free; and

WHEREAS, African Americans who had
been slaves in the Southwest celebrated June
19, commonly known as Juneteenth
Independence Day, as the anniversary of their
emancipation; and

WHEREAS, African Americans from
the Southwest continue the tradition of
Juneteenth Independence Day as inspiration
and encouragement for future generations; and

WHEREAS, for 140 years Juneteenth
Independence Day celebrations have been held
to honor African American freedom while
encouraging self-development and respect for
all cultures; and
WHEREAS, although Juneteenth
Independence Day is beginning to be
recognized as a national, and even global, event,
the history behind the celebration should not
be forgotten; and
WHEREAS, the faith and strength of
character demonstrated by former slaves
remains an example for all people of the United
States, regardless of background, religion, or
race;
NOW THEREFORE BE IT
RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of
the City of St. Louis, that we join with the
Juneteenth Independence Day Committee and
all of the citizens of the City of St. Louis in
celebrating Juneteenth Independence Day and
we encourage the meaningful participation in
the Juneteenth events occurring throughout
the St. Louis area and we further direct the
Clerk of this Board to spread a copy of this
Resolution across the minutes of these
proceedings and to prepare a commemorative
copy for presentation at a time and place
deemed appropriate by the Sponsor.
Introduced on the 17th day of June, 2005 by:
Honorable Terry Kennedy, Alderman 18th Ward
