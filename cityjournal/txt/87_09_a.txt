Board Bill No. 85
An ordinance approving a redevelopment
plan for the 1359 Belt Avenue Area (“Area”)
after finding that the Area is blighted as defined
in Section 99.320 of the Revised Statutes of
Missouri, 2000, as amended, (the “Statute”
being Sections 99.300 to 99.715 inclusive),
containing a description of the boundaries of
said Area in the City of St. Louis (“City”),
attached hereto and incorporated herein as
Exhibit “A”, finding that redevelopment and
rehabilitation of the Area is in the interest of
the public health, safety, morals and general
welfare of the people of the City; approving
the Blighting Study and Plan dated
April 27, 2004 for the Area (“Plan”),
incorporated herein by attached Exhibit “B”,
pursuant to Section 99.430; finding that there
is a feasible financial plan for the
redevelopment of the Area which affords
maximum opportunity for redevelopment of
the Area by private enterprise; finding that no
property in the Area may be acquired by the
Land Clearance for Redevelopment Authority
of the City of St. Louis (“LCRA”) through
the exercise of eminent domain, finding that
the property within the Area is unoccupied,
but if it should become occupied the
Redeveloper shall be responsible for relocating

any eligible occupants displaced as a result of
implementation of the Plan; finding that
financial aid may be necessary to enable the
Area to be redeveloped in accordance with
the Plan; finding that there shall be available
ten (10) year real estate tax abatement; and
pledging cooperation of the St. Louis Board
of Aldermen (“Board”) and requesting various
officials, departments, boards and agencies of
the City to cooperate and to exercise their
respective powers in a manner consistent with
the Plan.

Resolution No. 55

WHEREAS , we have been apprised that
after 18 years of dedicated service as an
educator at St. Raphael the Archangel School,
Noeleen M. F. Hannigan has announced her
retirement; and

WHEREAS , Ms. Hannigan was born
and raised in County Donegal, Ireland, one of
four children born to John and Winifred
Hannigan, and came to the United States in
1954 to join the Sisters of Charity of the
Incarnate Word of San Antonio, Texas; and

WHEREAS , Ms. Hannigan is a
distinguished graduate of Incarnate Word
College, in San Antonio, Texas, the University
of Missouri – St. Louis and St. Louis
University, where she received her Masters
of Divinity degree; and

WHEREAS , Ms. Hannigan served in the
education ministry of the Sisters of Charity
of the Incarnate Work for more than twentyseven years and served as an educator and
principal in a number of schools in Texas,
Illinois and Missouri prior to her tenure at St.
Raphael; and

WHEREAS, we are certain that Ms.
Hannigan is looking forward to a well deserved
retirement with her family and her many
friends;

NOW THEREFORE BE IT
RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of
the City of St. Louis that we pause in our
deliberations to congratulate Noeleen M. F.
Hannigan for her many years of service to the
St. Louis community and we wish her peace
and happiness in her retirement and we direct
the Clerk of this Board to spread a copy of

this Resolution across the minutes of these
proceedings and to further prepare a
commemorative copy to the end that it may
be presented to our honoree at a time and
place deemed appropriate by the Sponsor.
Introduced on the 21st day of May, 2004 by:
Honorable Donna Baringer, Alderwoman 16th Ward

Resolution No. 56
WHEREAS, on this 21st day of
May, 2004 we pause in our deliberations to
honor and welcome, to the City of St. Louis,
Simbi Vkek Mubako, Ambassador of the
Republic of Zimbabwe to the United States;
and
WHEREAS, Dr. Mubako, who was born
and raised in Zimbabwe, is a distinguished
graduate of the University of South Africa
and has been awarded numerous degrees from
the University College of Dublin, the London
School of Economics, Harvard University,
Knightsbridge University, United Kingdom
and the University of Olso; and
WHEREAS, prior to his career in service
to the government of Zimbabwe, Dr. Mubako
was a professor of law and taught and lectured
at universities throughout the world including
various universities in the United States; and
WHEREAS, in 1980, at the time of
Zimbabwe’s Independence, Dr. Mubako was
appointed Minister of Justice and
Constitutional Affairs and became an
outspoken advocate for legal reforms designed
to protect and promote the rights of women
and children; and
WHEREAS, in 1999, after having
served as Judge of the High Court for six years,
Dr. Mubako was appointed Ambassador to
the United States; and
WHEREAS , on Friday, May 21, 2004
Dr. Mubako will be honored at a reception in
the City of St. Louis hosted by the Universal
African People Organization at the fabulous
home of noted Attorney Donnell Smith;
NOW THEREFORE BE IT
RESOLVED, by this Board of Aldermen
that the City of St. Louis is greatly honored
by the visit of Ambassador Simbi Veke
Mubako and we congratulate him for his many
achievements as an government leader and
international statesman and wish him
continued success for many years, and we
direct the Clerk of this Board to prepare a
commemorative copy of this Resolution to
the end that it may be presented to our honoree
at a time and place deemed appropriate by
the Sponsor.

Inc. was founded on January 13, 1913, by
twenty-two collegiate women at Howard
University; and

WHEREAS, originally chartered as
Lambda Sigma, the St. Louis Alumnae Chapter
of Delta Sigma Theta has been committed to
public service in St. Louis for 77 years; and

WHEREAS , the first public act
performed by the Delta founders involved
their participation in the historic women’s
suffrage march in Washington D. C., in March
1913; and

WHEREAS , every Delta uses her
influence for the enactment of laws to protect

the unfortunate and for the repeal of those
laws which deprive human beings of their
privileges and rights; and
WHEREAS, Delta is increasingly
involved in important local and global issues
and recently received special consultative
status with the United Nations; and
WHEREAS, Deltas from across the
nation will travel to Washington D.C. to
participate in “Delta Days in the Nation’s
Capital” and Deltas from across the State
will travel to Jefferson City, Missouri to
participate in “Delta Days in the State
Capital”; and
WHEREAS, on May 21, 2004, Deltas
throughout the City of St. Louis will gather in
the Rotunda of City Hall to celebrate “ Delta
Day in St. Louis”;
NOW THEREFORE BE IT
RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of
the City of St. Louis, that we pause in our
deliberations to recognize and honor the
members of the St. Louis Chapter of Delta
Sigma Theta and we thank them for their many
contributions to our community and their
continuing commitment to the City of St. Louis
and we further direct the Clerk of this Board
