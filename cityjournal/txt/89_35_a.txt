(Floor Substitute)

An ordinance pertaining to the hiring of
probationary fire privates for the City of St.
Louis; providing that no person shall be
permitted to apply for the position of
firefighter if such person is 33 years of age or
over as of the last date that applications are
accepted by the Personnel Department for
such position ; containing an exception for
military service.

clause.

an emergency clause.

Board Bill No. 263

An ordinance, recommended by the
Board of Estimate and Apportionment,
authorizing the Mayor of the City of St. Louis,
on behalf of the City, to submit a 2007 Annual
Plan to the United States Department of
Housing and Urban Development (“HUD”)
as required to apply for funding under the
Federal Community Development Block
Grant (“CDBG”), HOME Investment
Partnerships (“HOME”), American Dream
Down Payment Initiative (“ADDI”),
Emergency Shelter Grant (“ESG”) and
Housing Opportunities for Persons with
AIDS (“HOPWA”) Entitlement Programs,
authorizing and directing the Mayor and the
Comptroller on behalf of the City to enter
into and execute agreements with HUD for
the receipt of 2007 CDBG, HOME, ADDI,
ESG and HOPWA funds, appropriating the
sum of Twenty-Two Million Eight Hundred
Twenty-Three Thousand Eight Hundred
Forty Dollars ($22,823,840) which the City
estimates will be available for the 2007 CDBG

Program Year, appropriating the sum of Four
Million Six Hundred Seven Thousand Dollars
($4,607,000) which the City estimates will
be available for the 2007 HOME Program Year,
appropriating the sum of Sixty-Eight
Thousand Dollars ($68,000) which the City
estimates will be available for the 2007 ADDI
Program Year, appropriating the sum of Two
Million Two Hundred Fifty Thousand Dollars
($2,250,000) which the City estimates will
be available from income generated by prior
year Urban Development Action Grant
(UDAG) projects; appropriating the sum of
One Million Twelve Thousand Dollars
($1,012,000) which the City estimates will
be available for the 2007 ESG Program Year,
appropriating the sum of One Million One
Hundred Eighty-Four Thousand Five
Hundred Dollars ($1,184,500) which the City
estimates will be available for the 2007
HOPWA Program Year, authorizing and
directing the Director of the Community
Development Administration (“CDA”) to
contract with municipal agencies, non-profit

WHEREAS, we have been apprised that
2006 marks 40 years of worldwide service of
the American Council of Young Political
Leaders (ACYPL) organization; and
WHEREAS, ACYPL was incorporated
on September 1, 1966, by young men and
women whose vision was to open lines of
communication among present and future
generations of political leadership; and
WHEREAS the ACYPL’s mission has
evolved from its initial focus on the interaction
between post World War II leadership in
Western Europe and the United States to the
global challenges facing the world today; and
WHEREAS today there are more than
six thousand ACYPL alumni worldwide,
many of whom have attained positions of
leadership and influence in their home
countries; and
WHEREAS, ACYPL sponsors
comprehensive study tours for young leaders
to give them valuable international exposure
early in their political careers; and
WHEREAS participants in ACYPL
programs represent a broad range of political,
social, cultural and geographic diversity; and
WHEREAS ACYPL remains steadfast
in its mission to promote understanding and
cultivate lasting political, economic and cultural
relationships among young political leaders
and policy-makers worldwide through the
regular exchange of delegations, educational
forums, leadership training and ongoing
dialogue;
NOW THEREFORE BE IT
RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of
the City of St. Louis that we recognize the
American Council of Young Political Leaders
organization for 40 years of service and we
thank them for their continuing effort to
improve political, social and cultural relations
throughout the world and we direct the Clerk
of this Board to prepare a commemorative
copy of this Resolution to the end that it may
be presented to our honorees at a time and
place deemed appropriate by the Sponsor.

in the building, designed by the revered
architect, William B. Ittner, at 3450 Wisconsin,
Saint Louis, Missouri; and
WHEREAS, the namesake for Shepard
Elementary School is Elihu Shepard, founder
of the Missouri Historical Society and member
of the first Saint Louis Board of Education;
and
WHEREAS, during the 1830’s Elihu
Shepard was one of the most active supporters
for establishing a public school system in Saint
Louis. Nearly 20 years later, he was
instrumental in persuading the Board to open
a school in the neighborhood of Marine
Hospital (current site of the U.S. Records
Center) as the first Shepard School. The
following year, Mr. Shepard donated to the
Board of Education a site on Marine Road
between Miami and Chippewa Streets for a
Shepard School. The first Shepard School was
opened in 1859, approximately in the area
where Marine Village Apartments are now
located; and
WHEREAS, during the school year
ended in June 1906 a new building at 3450
Wisconsin was completed. Over 1,000
children attended Shepard School that year;
and

WHEREAS, Shepard School celebrates
the three Accelerated School Principles of
unity of purpose, building on strengths, and
empowerment with responsibility; and is
committed to the purpose of educating all
students to a high level of academic
performance while fostering positive growth
in social and emotional behavior and attitude;
and
WHEREAS, Shepard School continues
to educate boys and girls in an exemplarly
manner. The school has won several awards:
Blue Ribbon School (1996); Gold Star School
(1997 & 2005). The students, staff, parents
and community continue to strive for a legacy
of mastery achievement, caring and diversity,
which they hope to extend for the next 100
years;
NOW THEREFORE BE IT
RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of
the City of St. Louis that we pause in our
deliberations to commemorate the 100 th
Birthday of Shepard Elementary School at
3450 Wisconsin. We congratulate the principal,
Carol Hall-Whittier, and the entire Shepard
School Family (administration, faculty, staff,
students, parents/guardians, alumni, and
community partners) for their contributions
to educational excellence in the Marine Villa
Neighborhood. We further direct the Clerk of
this Board to spread a copy of this Resolution
