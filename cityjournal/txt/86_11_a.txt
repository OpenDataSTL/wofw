Board Bill No. 123

An ordinance approving a redevelopment
plan for the 4246 Dewey Avenue Area (“Area”)
after finding that the Area is blighted as defined
in Section 99.320 of the Revised Statutes of
Missouri, 2000, as amended, (the “Statute”
being Sections 99.300 to 99.715 inclusive),
containing a description of the boundaries of
said Area in the City of St. Louis (“City”),
attached hereto and incorporated herein as
Exhibit “A”, finding that redevelopment and
rehabilitation of the Area is in the interest of
the public health, safety, morals and general
welfare of the people of the City; approving
the Blighting Study and Plan dated May 27,
2003 for the Area (“Plan”), incorporated herein
by attached Exhibit “B”, pursuant to Section
99.430; finding that there is a feasible financial
plan for the redevelopment of the Area which
affords maximum opportunity for
redevelopment of the Area by private
enterprise; finding that no property in the Area
may be acquired by the Land Clearance for
Redevelopment Authority of the City of St.
Louis (“LCRA”) through the exercise of
eminent domain; finding that the property
within the Area is unoccupied, but if it should
become occupied the Redeveloper shall be
responsible for relocating any eligible
occupants displaced as a result of
implementation of the Plan; finding that
financial aid may be necessary to enable the
Area to be redeveloped in accordance with
the Plan; finding that there shall be available
ten (10) year real estate tax abatement; and
pledging cooperation of the St. Louis Board
of Aldermen (“Board”) and requesting various
officials, departments, boards and agencies of
the City to cooperate and to exercise their
respective powers in a manner consistent with
the Plan.

WHEREAS, we have been apprised that

Take Five Magazine; and

media outlet for the African American

WHEREAS , as Managing Editor,

and her intolerance of injustice and corruption
provided the outline by which Take Five
Magazine presented community issues from
a unique and pro-active perspective; and

newspaper that provided in-depth, honest and

throughout the St. Louis region;

Sylvester and Victoria will continue to be
outspoken advocates for the African American
community in this City and will be a source
of insightful and responsible commentary on
the many important issues facing our citizens;

RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of
the City of St. Louis, that we pause in our
deliberations to recognize Sylvester Brown,
Jr. Victoria M. Anton-Brown and all of the
men and women who have contributed to the
success of Take Five Magazine and we thank
them for their outstanding contribution to the
St. Louis community and we further direct
the Clerk of this Board to spread a copy of
this Resolution across the minutes of these
proceedings and to prepare a commemorative
copy for presentation to our honorees at a
time and place deemed appropriate by the
Sponsor.

Resolution No. 70

WHEREAS, under the provisions of
Article I, Section 1(33) of the City Charter,
the City of St. Louis has a duty to do all
things whatsoever expedient for promoting
or maintaining the comfort, education ,
morals, peace, government, health, welfare,

trade, commerce or manufactures of the city
or its citizens; and
WHEREAS , recently the St. Louis City
School Board awarded a $5 Million contract
to Alvarez and Marsal, a private management
company based in New York, to act as interim
manager of the District for the purpose of
reorganizing the City Public School System;
and
WHEREAS, at a time when Federal,
State, and local budgets are being cut, the
wisdom of such a contract seems questionable
and the process by which it was awarded has
left many unanswered questions; and
WHEREAS, the School Board’s handling
of this matter has occurred, for the most part,
outside of the view of St. Louis City taxpayers
and the citizens of this community, whose
children will be directly impacted by this
decision, have a right to full disclosure of the
Board’s motives and plans; and
WHEREAS, it is within the purvey of
the Intergovernmental Affairs Committee of
the Board of Aldermen to conduct such inquiry
on behalf of our citizens;
NOW THEREFORE BE IT
RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of
the City of St. Louis, that we are direct the
Intergovernmental Affairs Committee to
convene public hearings for the purpose of
reviewing the actions of the St. Louis Public
School Board in awarding a management
contract to Alvarez and Marsal; such review
should include an examination of the Request
for Proposals issued by the School Board, a
copy of the contract with Alvarez and Marsal,
the testimony of St. Louis School Board
President Darnetta Clinckscale and William
V. Roberti, Managing Director of Alvarez and
Marsal and all other public documents and
witnesses deemed appropriate by the
Committee. For purposes of these hearings,
the Intergovernmental Affairs Committee shall
be authorized to issue subpoenas for the
production of documents and witnesses.
Introduced on the 6th day of June, 2003 by:

Honorable Freeman Bosley, Sr., Alderman 3rd Ward
