Board Bill No. 212
(Committee Substitute)
An ordinance pertaining to noise;
repealing Paragraph (7) of Section One of
Ordinance 41720, approved on January 4,
1940, and codified as Section 15.50.080 of
the Revised Code, and enacting in lieu thereof
a new ordinance prohibiting construction,
demolition and excavating within one thousand
(1,000) feet of a residential property, including
excavation, demolition, alteration or repair of
any building, land clearing, land grading or road
and utility construction within one thousand
(1,000) feet of a residential property before
6:00 a.m. and after 11:00 p.m., Monday
through Friday, and before 6:00 a.m. and after
12 Midnight, Saturday and Sunday, except in
case of an urgent necessity in the interest of
public safety; containing definitions and an
emergency clause.

containing an emergency clause.

Board Bill No. 81
(Committee Substitute)
An ordinance repealing Ordinance 50038
and enacting a new ordinance pertaining to
prohibiting installing, possessing or operating
in any motor vehicle in the City of St. Louis

any device for the producing or reproducing
of sound at any time with louder volume than
is necessary for convenient, normal hearing
for the persons who are in the vehicle in which
the device is being operated and who are
voluntary listeners: further prohibiting the
operating of any device for the producing or
reproducing of sound at any time with louder
volume than is necessary for convenient,
normal hearing for the persons who are in the
room in which the device is being operated
and who are voluntary listeners; containing
penalty provisions, including fines; seizure
and impoundment of vehicles for evidence;
and containing an emergency clause.
Alderman Kennedy
Chairman of the Committee

Resolution No. 243
WHEREAS, we pause in our
deliberations to note the untimely passing of
prominent St. Louisan and noted jurist, the
Honorable Theodore McMillian on January
18, 2006; and
WHEREAS, Judge McMillian was born
and raised in St. Louis, the eldest son of 10
children born to Theodore and Joycie Ann
McMillian and was a distinguished graduate
of Lincoln University and St. Louis University
School of Law; and
WHEREAS, Judge McMillian began his
outstanding legal career in 1949 as a founding
partner in the law firm of Lynch and
McMillian; and
WHEREAS, his career as a respected
jurist began in 1956 with his appointment to
the Circuit Bench for the Twenty-Second
Judicial Circuit in the City of St. Louis and
continued for more than 55 years in a variety
of positions including the Missouri Court of
Appeals for the Eastern District of Missouri
and the Eighth Circuit of the United States
Court of Appeals; and
WHEREAS, throughout his life of
service Judge McMillian was actively
involved in numerous community and
charitable organizations including, the National
Council of Juvenile Court Judges, the National
Council of Crime and Delinquency, the Urban
League of Metropolitan St. Louis, the St. Louis
Minority Economic Development Agency and
Catholic Charities of the City of St. Louis;
and
WHEREAS, Judge McMillian will be
greatly missed by his daughters, Cheryl and
Donna; his three grandchildren; his family and
his countless friends and admirers;
NOW THEREFORE BE IT
RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of
the City of St. Louis, that, on behalf of the St.
Louis community, we pause to remember the
many contributions of the Honorable
Theodore McMillian to the citizens of the
City of St. Louis, and we join with his many
friends in expressing our sorrow at his passing,
and we further direct the Clerk of this Board
to spread a copy of this Resolution across the
minutes of these proceedings and to prepare a
memorial copy for presentation to the
McMillian family, at a time and place deemed
appropriate by the Sponsor.
Introduced on the 10th day of February, 2006
by:

Honorable Michael McMillan,Alderman 19th Ward
