Board Bill No. 279
An ordinance approving a blighting study
and redevelopment plan dated October 27,
2009 for the 6027 Westminster Pl.
Redevelopment Area (as further defined herein,
the “Plan”) after finding that said
Redevelopment Area (“Area”) is blighted as
defined in Section 99.320 of the Revised
Statutes of Missouri, as amended (the
“Statute” being Sections 99.300 to 99.715
RSMo inclusive, as amended); containing a
description of the boundaries of the Area in
the City of St. Louis (“City”), attached hereto
and incorporated herein as Attachment “A”,
finding that redevelopment and rehabilitation
of the Area is in the interest of the public
health, safety, morals and general welfare of
the people of the City; approving the Plan
attached hereto and incorporated herein as
Attachment “B”, pursuant to Section 99.430
RSMo, as amended; finding that there is a
feasible financial plan for the redevelopment
of the Area which affords maximum
opportunity for redevelopment of the Area
by private enterprise; finding that no property
in the Area may be acquired by the Land
Clearance for Redevelopment Authority of the
City of St. Louis (“LCRA”), a public body
corporate and politic created under Missouri
law, through the exercise of eminent domain;
finding that the property within the Area is
occupied, and the Redeveloper(s) (as defined
herein) shall be responsible for providing
relocation assistance pursuant to the Plan to
any eligible occupants displaced as a result of
implementation of the Plan; finding that
financial aid may be necessary to enable the
Area to be redeveloped in accordance with
the Plan; finding that there shall be available
up to a five (5) year real estate tax abatement;
and pledging cooperation of this St. Louis
Board of Aldermen (“Board”) and requesting
various officials, departments, boards and
agencies of the City to cooperate and to
exercise their respective powers in a manner
consistent with the Plan; and containing a
severability clause.

Resolution No. 332

Caretta Darden
WHEREAS, we pause in our
deliberations to note the untimely passing of
Caretta Darden; and

WHEREAS, Caretta Darden was born
July 6, 1950 to the union of Lee and Delores
Warren; and

WHEREAS, Caretta attended and
received the sacraments of Baptism, First
Communion and Confirmation at St.
Mathews Catholic School; and

WHEREAS, Caretta had an enduring
bond with her step-father Colonel Hampton
Mays (Nick) who preceded her in death; and

WHEREAS, Caretta went on to Central
High School and enhanced her education thru
various training and enrichment opportunities;
and
WHEREAS, Caretta was athletic and a
musician in the truest form; and

WHEREAS, Caretta had lungs of steel
and enjoyed playing the soprano horn as she
traveled the United States in competitions
with the Lancer’s Drum & Bugle Corp; and

WHEREAS, Caretta was mother to five
children: Ramon L. W right, (preceded her in
death), Rona Wright-Taylor, Ronda WrightBroderick, Julius James III and Aisha James
and enjoyed being granny to Kaira, Nicolas,
Raiven, Ramon II, Layla, Lacy, Julianna,
Kenya, Nasir, Amir and Kair; and

WHEREAS, prior to her illness
progression, Caretta was an inactive Board
Member of the Lancer’s Drum & Bugle Corp;
and

WHEREAS, in her professional life
Caretta gained numerous acknowledgements
and certifications in her professional
endeavors; and

WHEREAS, in her lifetime Caretta’s
talents contributed to numerous small,
minority businesses growth and sustainability;
and

WHEREAS, Caretta was employed
with Bank of America as Senior Officer
Operations Analyst and had a gift of building
instant rapport with her customers; and

WHEREAS, Caretta fought a great
battle with breast cancer and was an advocate
for breast cancer research; and

WHEREAS, Caretta’s zest for life was
contagious and she was loved by many and
will be missed greatly by her family.

NOW THEREFORE BE IT
RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of
the City of St. Louis that we pause to
remember the many contributions of Caretta
Darden and we join with her many friends in
expressing our sorrow at her passing and we
further direct the Clerk of this Board to spread
a copy of this Resolution across the minutes
of these proceedings and to prepare a memorial
copy for presentation to the Darden family,
at a time and place deemed appropriate by
the Sponsor.

Introduced on the 5th day of March, 2010
by:
Honorable Charles QuincyTroupe,Alderman 1stWard

Resolution No. 334
Community Health-In-Partnership
Services (CHIPS)
WHEREAS, the City of St. Louis is
home to some of the Nation’s most highly
regarded hospitals and health centers, the low
income uninsured population in the City of
St. Louis continues to lack access to
healthcare; and
WHEREAS, Community Health-InPartnership Services (CHIPS) is a community
based not-for-profit health and wellness center
founded in 1990 to provide healthcare and
social services to uninsured residents of the
City of St. Louis, with the vision that every
member of the community be assured access
to available and affordable healthcare and
social services that ensures the continued
development of a productive, healthy
community; and
WHEREAS, CHIPS is dedicated to its
mission of promoting wellness and
empowering self health management for the
uninsured and underserved through preventive
and primary healthcare services. In support
of this mission, CHIPS partners with
volunteer
Physicians,
Dentists,
Chiropractors, Optometrists, Dietitians and
other professionals to provide services that
include Internal Medicine, Gynecology,
Pediatrics, Dermatology, Optometry,
Chiropractic, Social Services and Behavioral
Health, In Home Care Services,
Complementary Health, Wellness Programs
and Support Groups. The CHIPS In
MOTION educational program uses the
performing arts to communicate health and
wellness principles to community groups. The
dynamic CLUB CHIPS Youth Leadership
Development Program trains high school
students to be peer health educators; and
WHEREAS, CHIPS is unique because
it not only provides primary care in a clinic
setting, but also incorporates community
outreach health screenings among its core
services. CHIPS does not wait for at risk
populations to ask for help. CHIPS brings
health screenings and health education to the
community to reach at risk individuals who
do not have access to healthcare. CHIPS
provides medical referrals to health care
centers to individuals with abnormal screening
results, and follows up to make sure they
access care.
NOW THEREFORE BE IT
RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of
the City of St. Louis that we pause in our
deliberations and offer congratulations and

best wishes to CHIPS for the dedicated and
superior health care they render to the less
fortunate members of our community and we
further direct the Clerk of this Board to spread
a copy of this Resolution across the minutes
of these proceedings and to prepare a
commemorative copy to the end that it may
be presented to our honorees at a time and
place deemed appropriate by the Sponsor.

Introduced on the 5th day of March, 2010
by:
Honorable Charles QuincyTroupe,Alderman 1stWard

Notice to All Suppliers
NOTE: It is the policy of the City of
St. Louis that all firms desiring to do business
with the City of St. Louis must comply with
employment practices that are in accordance
with the ordinances of the City of St. Louis
and the Mayor’s Executive Order promulgated
there-under to the end that all Contractors
and suppliers of materials and services will
offer equal opportunity for employment and
job advancement to blacks and other minority
groups in the St. Louis Metropolitan area.

Requesting a Bid
To have a bid mailed or faxed to you,
please contact the Supply Commissioner’s
office at (314) 622-4580.
Bidders are invited to be present at the
opening of bids. Bid results may be available
30 days following the date of bid opening.
If you desire bid results, please include a
self-address, stamped envelope with your bid.
The City of St. Louis desires to
purchase recycled products whenever
possible, especially items containing post
consumer waste materials. If your company
can supply recycled products, please provide
information to this office.

Surplus Property
Notice is hereby given that the City of

during the course of the year. The property
may be listed at www.govdeals.com or in the
City Journal.
The right to reject any and all bids is
reserved.
