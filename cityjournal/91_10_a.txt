Resolution No. 62
Officer Sean Lipina
Whereas, we have been apprised that
Officer Sean Lipina was unanimously selected
as the St. Louis Metropolitan Police
Department Sixth District Officer of the Year;
and
Whereas, Officer Lipina entered the
academy on July 11, 2005 and was
commissioned on February 26, 2006. He is
currently assigned to the North Patrol
Division, 6th District night watch. He has
received the awards of Officer of the Month
for the month of May in 2007 and a letter of
accommodation from the Captain (Burglary
arrest); and
Whereas, Randy and Deagnes Lipina
are the proud parents of this officer, who have
always been enthusiastic and supportive. He
has a younger brother, Ryan Lipina, who is
currently studying electrical engineering at
SIUE and an older and wiser sister, Kim
Lestinski, who is the ideal mother of six living
in Phoenix, Arizona; and
Whereas, Officer Lipina has
demonstrated a consistent professional model
of a police officer. Officer Lipina remains
prompt for his tour of duty sharp in
appearance; and
Whereas, Officer Lipina approaches
his assigned tasks with great concern for the
community’s safety and progressive
development. Officer Lipina is frequently
sought after by his peers for guidance and
assistance with various assignments and
projects. Officer Lipina is proactive in that
he constantly seeks out problem areas and
develops an action plans for resolutions
without direction; and
Whereas, Officer Lipina is committed
to preserving the peace and safety of the
citizens in that he led the district by making
109 arrests with a total of 313 charges, 74
being felonies. Because of his overall
awareness, strong initiative and dedication to
the community, Officer Lipina removed
several criminals from the neighborhoods and
helped create a safer environment.
NOW

THEREFORE

BE

IT

RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of
the City of St. Louis that we pause in our
deliberations to recognize and congratulate
Officer Sean Lipina for the award of Officer
of the Year for the Sixth District and we wish
him continued success and achievement and
we further direct the Clerk of this Board to
spread a copy of this Resolution across the
minutes of these proceedings and to prepare a
commemorative copy to the end that it may
be presented to our honoree at a time and
place deemed appropriate by the Sponsor.

Introduced on the 23rd day of May, 2008 by

Resolution No. 63

WHEREAS, we pause in our
deliberations to note the untimely passing of
longtime St. Louis resident, Mr. Leon Mix;
and

WHEREAS, Mr. Leon Mix was born
on December 31, 1951 in St. Louis, Missouri
to the late Tard and Frances Mix. He was the
fifth of ten children. Of the ten children,
Beatrice and Rodney preceded him in death.
He departed this life on March 22, 2008 at his
home to be with his Heavenly Father in His
Kingdom; and

WHEREAS, Leon attended Patrick
Henry Grade School and O’Fallon Tech High.
He graduated from Central High School in 1969.
He attended Northeast Missouri State College
for 2 ½ years; and

WHEREAS, Leon worked at Hardy
Men Show Store as a manager, Southwestern
Bell Telephone Company as a Sales
Representative and worked for the City
Workhouse as a Correctional Officer for 20
years. In 1975, he went into business for
himself by owning Albu Music Shop, a year
later he expanded his shop to the 14th Street
Mall and named it Hudson Embry Shop; and

WHEREAS, Leon started coaching, in
1976, little league football and basketball. He
was a pillar of the community. He loved
children and was a family person. His home
was a welcome door. He liked to cook, music,
and D.J. He was a fun person; he liked
partying and barbecued all the time. He was
an inspiration to his children and other people,
especially to the boys; and

WHEREAS, Leon leaves to cherish his
ex-wife, Sharon (Mix) Cole; four children,
Dwjuan, Damita, Dion, Damarr and Leon Mix
in San Antonio, Texas, a Staff Sgt. serving in
the U.S. Air Force; one daughter-in-law,

Stacey; four grandsons, Jerry, Jeremiah,
DaShawn and Dwjuan; two brothers, LeRoy
Mix and Alvin Mix; five sisters, Schryl Taylor,
Flora Mix, LaVern Neal, Arlene Neal and Anita
Mix; six brother-in-laws, Michael Neal, Mark
Taylor, Harold Small, Charles Thomas, Alfred
Robinson and Orville Jackson; four sister-inlaws, Lois Gates, Gwen Stevenson, Sheila
Downs and Cynthia Robinson; ex-mother-inlaw, Willa Mae Brown; and special loving
Godson, Steve Goodlow.
NOW THEREFORE BE IT
RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of
the City of St. Louis that we pause to
remember the many contributions of Leon Mix
to the citizens of the City of St. Louis and we
join with his many friends in expressing our
sorrow at his passing, and we further direct
the Clerk of this Board to spread a copy of
this Resolution across the minutes of these
proceedings and to prepare a memorial copy
for presentation to the Mix family, at a time
and place deemed appropriate by the Sponsor
Introduced on the 23rd day of May, 2008 by:

better and more interesting place to live.
