      Board of Aldermen Report, December

      To the President of the Board of        
 Aldermen:                                    
I wish to report that on the 18th day of            
November, 2005, I delivered the Office of the       
Mayor of the City of St. Louis the following        
board bills that were truly agreed to and finally   
adopted.                                            
           Board Bill No. 269                       
       An      ordinance        approving        a  
Redevelopment Plan for 4012, 4015, 4016,            
4018, 4019, 4022, 4025, 4030, 4036, 4044,           
4050, & 4054 Lincoln Avenue Area ("Area")           
after finding that the Area is blighted as defined  
in Section 99.320 of the Revised Statutes of        
Missouri, 2000, as amended, (the "Statute"          
being Sections 99.300 to 99.715 inclusive),         
containing a description of the boundaries of       
said Area in the City of St. Louis ("City"),        
attached hereto and incorporated herein as          
Exhibit "A", finding that redevelopment and         
rehabilitation of the Area is in the interest of    
the public health, safety, morals and general       
welfare of the people of the City; approving        
the Plan dated August 23, 2005 for the Area         
("Plan"), incorporated herein by attached           
Exhibit "B", pursuant to Section 99.430;            
finding that there is a feasible financial plan     
for the development of the Area which affords       
maximum opportunity for development of the          
Area by private enterprise; finding that no         
property in the Area may be acquired by the         
Land Clearance for Redevelopment Authority          
of the City of St. Louis ("LCRA") through           
the exercise of eminent domain; finding that        
the property within the Area is unoccupied,         
but if it should become occupied the                
Redeveloper shall be responsible for relocating     
any eligible occupants displaced as a result of     
implementation of the Plan; finding that            
financial aid may be necessary to enable the        
Area to be redeveloped in accordance with           
the Plan; finding that there shall be available     
ten (10) year real estate tax abatement; and        
pledging cooperation of the Board of                
Aldermen and requesting various officials,          
departments, boards and agencies of the City        
to cooperate and to exercise their respective       
powers in a manner consistent with the Plan.

 honoree, Ralph Bunch; and                         
      WHEREAS, Mr. Franklin moved to St.           
 Louis in 1936 and in 1938 began his fifty year    
 career in education in the St. Louis Public       
 School System, teaching first at Vashon High      
 School and, beginning in 1963 and ending at       
 Cleveland High School; and                        
       WHEREAS, Mr. Franklin was a man of          
 great intelligence, thought and wisdom who        
 had an honest and sincere interest in the         
 betterment of the St. Louis community, was        
 keenly devoted to his students and worked         
 tirelessly to secure social and economic          
 equality and justice for teachers and             
 disenfranchised African Americans in the St.      
 Louis community; and                              
      WHEREAS, Mr. Franklin was an                 
 outstanding member of our community               
providing energy, vision and leadership to the     
Union Sarah Community Corporation,                 
SLACO, the Union Sarah Economic                    
Development Corporation, Boy Scout Troop           
124 of Lane Tabernacle Church and countless        
other community organizations, ventures and        
efforts; and                                       
      WHEREAS, Mr. Franklin, will be greatly       
missed by his wife, Verna; his children, Joyce,    
Paula, Richard and Michael; his eleven             
grandchildren; his seven great-grandchildren;      
his great great granddaughter and his countless    
friends, former students and admirers;             
       NOW THEREFORE BE IT                         
RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of               
the City of St. Louis, that, on behalf of the St.  
Louis community, we pause to remember the          
many contributions of Bostic J. Franklin to        
the citizens of the City of St. Louis, and we      
join with his many friends in expressing our       
sorrow at his passing though celebrating his       
life filled with many achievements and we          
further direct the Clerk of this Board to spread   
a copy of this Resolution across the minutes       
of these proceedings and to prepare a memorial     
copy for presentation to the Franklin family,      
at a time and place deemed appropriate by          
the Sponsor.
