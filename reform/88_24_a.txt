PROGRAM SPECIFICATIONS                            
      The City of St. Louis is seeking to enter   
into a Twelve (12) month contract beginning       
Oct 1, 2005 with the option to extend for one     
(1) additional year.                              
RATIONALE                                         
     The Animal Regulation Center of the          
City of St. Louis is a provider of service to     
the general public providing animal bite          
investigation/observation, stray animal           
apprehension, animal adoptions, rabies            
vaccination/registration and other animal         
control related functions. The Department of      
Health and Hospitals operates the Animal          
Regulation Center and provides housing and        
humane treatment to over 7,000 reported dogs,     
cats and other animals and reptiles.              
Veterinarian services are needed to support       
these activities.

 E. SCOPE OF WORK                               
      The Contractor must maintain all          
 necessary licenses to fulfill the obligations  
 under this contract.                           
      -Contractor will observe all animals      
 brought into the Animal Regulation Center or   
 any other location requested by the City with  
 emphasis on those animals deemed to be biting  
 animals.                                       
      -Contractor will administer vaccine to    
 animals as deemed appropriate by the City or   
 the Contractor.                                
      -Contractor will prescribe, diagnose and  
 provide topical treatment to all animals as    
 necessary. Will perform necessary euthanasia   
 when needed.                                   
       -Contractor, upon request of the City,   
 will participate in professional consultation  
 to the Animal Regulation Center staff.         
       -Contractor will visit the Animal        
 Regulation Center Monday through Friday        
 and participate in no less than 30 minutes of  
 professional services between the hours of     
 8:30 a.m. and 9:30 a.m. and 30 minutes of      
 professional services between the hours of     
 3:00 p.m. and 4:00 p.m. Contractor will visit  
 the Animal Regulation Center every Saturday    
 and participate in no less than 30 minutes of  
 professional services between the hours of     
 8:30 a.m. and 9:30 a.m. All of the above hours 
 may be changed by mutual consent of the        
 parties. Contractor shall also be available for
 emergency treatment (during regular business   
 hours and on call after hours) contact or      
 consultation.-III.                             
       -Contractor will provide euthanasia-by-  
 injection services four days a week mornings   
 between the hours of 7:30 a.m. and 8:30 a.m.   
 at the City of St. Louis Animal Regulation     
 Center at 2120 Gasconade Street, St. Louis,    
 MO 63118.                                      
      -Contractor will euthanize those animals  
 as determined by, and at the direction and     
 supervision of, the Animal Regulation staff.   
 Per policies and procedures of the Animal      
 Regulation Center.
