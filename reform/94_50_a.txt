           Resolution No. 312                    
     Superintendent Buster Ollie                 
      WHEREAS,WHEREAS, Superinten-               
 dent Buster Ollie has been a pastor in the      
 Wellston community for 36 years. He has         
 helped proclaim the Gospel to the lost and      
 has ministered to the total man at heart. He    
 has kept the community strong by helping        
 young gang members, helping homeless            
 families and those struggling with drug         
 addictions; and                                 
       WHEREAS, these are the same people        
 who come and sit in the back of the church on   
 a Tuesday, Wednesday, or Friday night and       
 listen as he ministers the words of God to      
 them both spiritually, mentally, economically   
 and socially. He has encouraged the believer    
 and non-believer in the community to have a     
 personal relationship with God though           
 restoration, worship, nurturing, godly living,  
 and teaching of one another; and                
       WHEREAS, Pastor Ollie has equipped        
 and allowed those to do true ministry for       
 community and the Kingdom of God. He            
 believes in sowing money and helping the men    
 trying to feed their families find jobs; and    
       WHEREAS, he has provided several          
 clothing giveaways, free back to school         
 supplies and hot meals to help feed the less    
 fortunate. He has even given money to assist    
 homeless individuals with a place to go for     
 the night; and                                  
      WHEREAS, Superintendent Ollie's            
 vision is to help build up the neighborhood     
 economically by creating child development      
 centers, adult daycare centers, youth outreach  
 centers, and a program to offer job readiness   
 skills which will hopefully lead to job         
 placement.                                      
      NOW THEREFORE BE IT                        
 RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of            
 the City of St. Louis that we pause in our      
 deliberations to recognize Superintendent       
 Buster Ollie and we wish him continued          
 success, achievement and accomplishment and     
 we further direct the Clerk of this Board to    
 spread a copy of this Resolution across the     
 minutes of these proceedings and to prepare a   
 commemorative copy to the end that it may       
 be presented to our honoree at a time and       
 place deemed appropriate by the Sponsor.        
 Introduced on the 24th day of February, 2012    
 by:                                             
 Honorable Samuel L. Moore,Alderman 4th Ward

Ordinance 68603, is seeking vendors for           
Downtown Ending District, which includes          
both the Downtown Vending District and the        
City parks located within the District. The       
vending location is guaranteed through            
December 31, 2015, provided the Vendor            
complies with all rules, including payment of     
the annual fee. The minimum annual fee for a      
Vendor's permit is $500 per year. A vendor is     
required to operate a minimum of 90 days per      
year for a minimum of 4 hours per day. The        
"Downtown Vending District" is the area           
bound by the east side of Fourth Street on the    
east, the centerlines of Cole Street on the       
north, Eighteenth Street on the west, and         
Chouteau Street on the south. Vendors shall       
be selected based upon the criteria set forth in  
the Solicitation for Permit Applications,         
which may be obtained from the Parks              
Department at 5600 Clayton Avenue, 314-           
289-5344, or the Streets Department at 1900       
Hampton Avenue, 314-647-3111, Sta. 109.           
The last day for filing applications for the      
Vending District is March 9, 2012.
