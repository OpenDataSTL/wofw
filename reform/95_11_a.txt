           Resolution No. 32                     
         Dr. Barbara Anderson                    
     WHEREAS, Dr. Barbara Anderson has           
served St. Louis Public Schools for 32 years.    
She first entered the St. Louis Public Schools   
at the Marshall Elementary school as a           
student. After completing her education at       
O'Fallon Technical High School (and              
McKinley High School), she began her college     
education and completed a Bachelor of Arts       
Degree from Harris Teacher's College. Dr.        
Anderson earned a Master of Arts in Teaching     
Degree from Webster University. She earned       
a Master of Arts in Administration Degree        
from St. Louis University. Dr. Anderson          
earned a Doctorate of Education Degree from      
St. Louis University in 2012; and                
      WHEREAS, Dr. Anderson began her            
teaching career at Clark 7th and 8th Grade       
Center. She spent ten years teaching in middle   
schools and then transitioned to Shaw Visual     
and Performing Arts Center, where she spent      
eight years teaching 1st grade; and              
     WHEREAS, after leaving Shaw, Dr.            
Anderson re-entered middle school and taught     
Language Arts at Busch Athletic Academic         
Academy. Then she left the regular classroom     
and became a Chapter I math teacher at           
Marquette Visual and Performing Arts Center,     
after which she again taught Math and            
Language Arts at Langston Middle School.         
After leaving Langston Middle, she entered       
administration as an Instructional Coordinator   
assigned to Elliot Elementary; and               
      WHEREAS, in 2004, Dr. Anderson was         
assigned to be Principal of Lexington            
Elementary School. She received an Apple         
for the Teacher Award in 2007 and a Pettus       
Teacher of Excellence Award in 2010. During      
her tenure at Lexington as principal, Lexington  
was listed as a "most improved school" by        
DESE in 2005; and                                
      WHEREAS, Lexington has achieved            
Adequate Yearly Progress (AYP) in all areas      
four times. Lexington has achieved the State     
Target on the MAP in communication arts          
five times. Currently and for the past three     
years, only the gifted school scored higher in   
communication arts; and                          
       WHEREAS, in 2009, Lexington was             
 honored with Monsanto's School of Excellence      
 Award; Secretary of Education Arne Duncan         
 was a visitor as was Missouri Senator Claire      
 McCaskill. Lexington has received national        
 recognition for excellence from Randi             
 Weingarten the national president of the AFT      
 Teachers' Union and from Arne Duncan for          
 collaborating with the labor union and having     
 a collegial staff.                                
       NOW THEREFORE BE IT                         
 RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of              
 the City of St. Louis that we pause in our to     
 honor Dr. Barbara Anderson and we further         
 direct the Clerk of this Board to spread a copy   
 of this Resolution across the minutes of these    
 proceedings and to prepare a commemorative        
 copy to the end that it may be presented to       
 our honorees at a time and place deemed           
 appropriate by the Sponsor.

            Resolution No. 33                      
    Consuelo Hopkins Wilkins, MD                   
       WHEREAS, Consuelo Hopkins                   
 Wilkins, MD is an associate professor in the      
 Department of Medicine, Division of               
 Geriatrics and Nutritional Science, with          
 secondary appointments in Psychiatry and          
 Surgery (Public Health Sciences) at the School    
 of Medicine at Washington University in St.       
 Louis. She currently serves as co-director of     
 the Center for Community Engaged Research         
 in the Institute of Clinical and Translational    
 Science, director of the Community Outreach       
 Program in the Nutrition Obesity Research         
 Center, and director of Our Community, Our        
 Health - a collaborative program with Saint       
 Louis University to disseminate culturally        
 relevant health information and facilitate        
 community-academic partnerships to address        
 health disparities. Dr. Wilkins has served as     
 Medical Director of Barnes-Jewish Extended        
 Care since 2001; and                              
      WHEREAS, Dr. Wilkins is also the             
 founding director of the Center for Community     
 Health and Partnerships at the Institute for      
 Public Health at Washington University. The       
 Office focuses on building and sustaining         
 mutually-beneficial community-academic            
 partnerships that effectively address the health  
 needs of the St. Louis community; and             
       WHEREAS, the primary focus of her           
 research has been on identifying and addressing   
 risk factors (such as socioeconomic status,       
 depression, weight, and vitamin D status) for     
 cognitive impairment in order adults. Dr.         
 Wilkins' research experience also includes a      
 spectrum of clinical and translational projects   
 including neuropathological studies of          
 dementia in African Americans, neuroimaging     
 studies in depressed older adults, and the      
 development of tools to assess functional       
 status in community-dwelling elderly; and       
      WHEREAS, in 2008, Dr. Wilkins              
 launched CARE in Our Community, a               
 transdisciplinary program, that uses            
 community-based team approach and               
 evidence-based interventions to improve         
 mobility, decrease falls and decrease depressive
 symptoms (using individualized problem          
 solving therapy). CARE is currently in its      
 second cycle of funding and has enrolled more   
 than 650 adults (65% live in HUD subsidized     
 housing and 82% are African American) ; and     
        WHEREAS, Dr. Wilkins is also firmly      
 committed to improving the health of under-     
 served and disadvantaged groups. She has        
 held offices in multiple professional and civic 
 organizations including President of the        
 Mound City Medical Forum (the St. Louis         
 affiliate of the National Medical Association), 
 Vice Chair for Region V of the National Medical 
 Association, and Chair of the City of St. Louis'
 Affordable Housing Commission which             
 administers grants and awards of more than      
 $4 million annually to support low-income       
 families. She also serves as the Health Editor  
 and a columnist of the nationally-recognized    
 St. Louis American Newspaper, which             
 reaches an audience of 200,000, mostly African
