       An      ordinance       approving        a
 Redevelopment Plan for the Lincoln Ave./        
 Cottage Ave./N. Sarah St. Area ("Area") after   
 finding that the Area is blighted as defined in 
 Section 99.320 of the Revised Statutes of       
 Missouri, 2000, as amended, (the "Statute"      
 being Sections 99.300 to 99.715 inclusive),     
 containing a description of the boundaries of   
 said Area in the City of St. Louis ("City"),    
 attached hereto and incorporated herein as      
 Exhibit "A", finding that redevelopment and     
 rehabilitation of the Area is in the interest of
 the public health, safety, morals and general   
 welfare of the people of the City; approving    
 the Plan dated March 27, 2007 for the Area      
 ("Plan"), incorporated herein by attached       
 Exhibit "B", pursuant to Section 99.430;        
 finding that there is a feasible financial plan 
 for the development of the Area which affords   
 maximum opportunity for development of the      
 Area by private enterprise; finding that no     
 property in the Area may be acquired by the     
 Land Clearance for Redevelopment Authority      
 of the City of St. Louis ("LCRA") through       
 the exercise of eminent domain; finding that    
 the property within the Area is unoccupied,     
 but if it should become occupied the            
 Redeveloper shall be responsible for relocating 
 any eligible occupants displaced as a result of 
 implementation of the Plan; finding that        
 financial aid may be necessary to enable the    
 Area to be redeveloped in accordance with       
 the Plan; finding that there shall be available 
 ten (10) year real estate tax abatement; and    
 pledging cooperation of the Board of            
 Aldermen and requesting various officials,      
 departments, boards and agencies of the City    
 to cooperate and to exercise their respective   
 powers in a manner consistent with the Plan.

            Resolution No. 58                        
     WHEREAS, This honorable Board of                
Aldermen of the City of St. Louis has been           
apprised of the upcoming 99 th birthday              
celebration of Mr. Joel "Joe" Sanders to be          
held at St. James AME Church this Sunday,            
May 27, 2007; and                                    
     WHEREAS, Elder Sanders was born                 
May 30, 1908 in Canton, Mississippi and              
was named at birth Joel Emmanuel Sanders             
but was called "Joe" by most of his relatives,       
friends and people in the community; and             
    WHEREAS, Mr. Sanders moved from                  
Canton, Mississippi to Gary, Indiana at the          
 age of 17 where he worked in the steel mills       
 and was married for a brief period there, then     
 later moved to St. Louis during the this           
 country's Great Depression; and                    
      WHEREAS, During WWII Mr. Sanders              
 worked as a freight handler for the Burlington     
 Railroad Company which was consider an             
 important job supporting the war effort and        
 kept him here in the United States during the      
 war; and                                           
       WHEREAS, After the war Mr. Sanders           
 became a maintenance worker at various             
 locations which was considered a stable and        
 significant job for African Americans during       
 the racially segregated years of the United        
 States; and                                        
       WHEREAS, In St. Louis Mr. Sanders            
 married his present able bodied and energetic      
 wife Mrs. Wanita Sanders in 1959 and has           
 three children, 8 grandchildren and 3 great grand  
 children having his first child at the age of 50;  
 and                                                
       WHEREAS, Mr. Sanders has been an             
 active member of the 18th Ward supporting          
 many events and participating in his block         
 unit association and other community effort        
 for many years making him both a pillar and        
 model for future generations and coining the       
 nickname the "swinger" due to all his activities   
 and his youthfulness; and                          
        WHEREAS, Mr. Sanders has lived nine         
 decades in this country living through, the        
 roaring twenties, the depression, the advent       
 of electrical home lighting, the Harlem            
 Renaissance, the first space travel, creation of   
 jet planes, the ending of legislated segregation,  
 the great migration of African Americans from      
 the south to northern cities, advances made        
 through the civil rights movement and many         
 other historical events that other generations     
 can only read about.                               
       NOW THEREFORE BE IT                          
 RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen for              
 the City of St. Louis that we pause in our         
 deliberations to recognize the 99th birthday       
 celebration of one our community elders Mr.        
 "Joe" Sanders "the Swinger" and by adoption        
 of this resolution wishes to join in the           
 festivities and congratulatory acts honoring       
 Mr. Sanders and we direct the Clerk of this        
 Board to prepare a commemorative copy of           
 this Resolution to the end that it may be          
 presented to our honoree at a time and place       
 deemed appropriate by the Sponsor.
