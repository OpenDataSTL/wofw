            Board Bill No. 137                    
       An Ordinance pertaining to the Health      
 Insurance Portability and Accountability Act     
 of 1996, P.L. 104-91 (hereinafter HIPAA);        
 repealing Ordinance 66281 and enacting in lieu   
 thereof a new ordinance providing for            
 compliance by the City with both the HIPAA       
 Standards for Privacy of Individually            
 Identifiable Health Information, 45 C.F.R. Part  
 160 and Part 164, Subparts A and E (the          
 Privacy Rule) and the HIPAA Security             
 Standards for the Protection of Electronic       
 Protected Health Information, 45 C.F.R. Part     
 160 and Part 164, Subparts A and C (the          
 Security Rule); re-designating the City's        
 Hybrid Entity Health Care Components, with       
 removal of the Health Department from such       
 designation; re-designating the City's Business  
 Associate Components, with removal of the        
 Department of Public Service Building            
 Division, and requiring Business Associate       
 Components to meet certain requirements of       
 the Privacy Rule and Security Rule;              
 appointing the City Privacy Officer and the      
 Privacy Officers for each of the designated      
 Health Care Components, providing for their      
 duties, and providing for amendments of such     
 designations; appointing the City Security       
 Officer and the Security Officers for each of    
 the designated Health Care Components,           
 providing for their duties, and providing for    
 amendments of such designations; authorizing     
 the Health Care Component Privacy Officers,      
 upon recommendation of the City Counselor,       
 to enter into Business Associate Agreements      
 in compliance with the Privacy Rule and the      
 Security Rule, and upon recommendation of        
 the City Counselor and the Board of Estimate     
 and Apportionment, to enter into all other       
 agreements required for compliance with the      
 Privacy Rule and the Security Rule; and          
 providing for a severability clause.

            Board Bill No. 137                  
       An Ordinance pertaining to the Health    
 Insurance Portability and Accountability Act   
 of 1996, P.L. 104-91 (hereinafter HIPAA);      
 repealing Ordinance 66281 and enacting in lieu 
 thereof a new ordinance providing for          
 compliance by the City with both the HIPAA     
 Standards for Privacy of Individually          
 Identifiable Health Information, 45 C.F.R. Part
 160 and Part 164, Subparts A and E (the        
 Privacy Rule) and the HIPAA Security           
 Standards for the Protection of Electronic     
 Protected Health Information, 45 C.F.R. Part   
 160 and Part 164, Subparts A and C (the        
 Security Rule); re-designating the City's      
 Hybrid Entity Health Care Components, with     
 removal of the Health Department from such     
 designation; re-designating the City's Business
 Associate Components, with removal of the      
 Department of Public Service Building          
 Division, and requiring Business Associate     
 Components to meet certain requirements of     
 the Privacy Rule and Security Rule;            
 appointing the City Privacy Officer and the    
 Privacy Officers for each of the designated    
 Health Care Components, providing for their    
 duties, and providing for amendments of such   
 designations; appointing the City Security     
 Officer and the Security Officers for each of  
 the designated Health Care Components,         
 providing for their duties, and providing for  
 amendments of such designations; authorizing   
 the Health Care Component Privacy Officers,    
 upon recommendation of the City Counselor,     
 to enter into Business Associate Agreements    
 in compliance with the Privacy Rule and the    
 Security Rule, and upon recommendation of      
 the City Counselor and the Board of Estimate   
 and Apportionment, to enter into all other     
 agreements required for compliance with the    
 Privacy Rule and the Security Rule; and        
 providing for a severability clause.           
            Board Bill No. 115                  
       An ordinance pertaining to street        
 vending within the Civic Center Vending        
 District; amending Section Four of Ordinance   
 65061 to designate the boundaries of the Civic 
 Center Vending District; establishing rules and
 regulations for Civic Center Vending District  
 and containing an emergency clause.

          Resolution No. 160                      
     WHEREAS, we pause in our                     
deliberations to note the untimely passing of     
longtime St. Louis resident, Bernice Dianne       
Smith; and                                        
      WHEREAS, Bernice Diane Smith was            
born October 20, 1944 in Coulterville, Illinois.  
She was the only child of Edward William          
Johnson and Nora Mae Oliver Johnson. Her          
grandmother Zelpha Oliver played a major          
role in her life; and                             
     WHEREAS, Bernice Dianne Smith,               
beloved by all who knew her, was always           
called Dianne by her family and those that        
knew her best; and                                
     WHEREAS, as Diane grew up in                 
Coulterville township and was educated and        
graduated from Coulterville High School and       
went on to attend Southern Illinois University;   
and                                               
     WHEREAS, Diane held various jobs,            
including 16 years at Carter Carburetor and       
for the last 21 years Diane served as             
administrative assistant to Wayman Smith, an      
executive at Anheuser-Busch Companies.            
Diane was the executive secretary to Johnny       
Furr, Director of Community Affairs and           
Urban Development at Anheuser-Busch until         
her untimely death; and                           
      WHEREAS, Diane's life was a testament       
of her belief in God. She was never afraid to     
reach out to help someone; she was vivacious      
and never met a stranger. She was a great         
community servant, wife and mother. She           
passed away September 10, 2008 and will be        
truly missed.                                     
      NOW THEREFORE BE IT                         
RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of              
the City of St. Louis that we pause to            
remember the many contributions of Bernice        
Dianne Smith to the citizens of the City of St.   
Louis and we join with her many friends in        
expressing our sorrow at her passing, and we      
further direct the Clerk of this Board to spread  
a copy of this Resolution across the minutes      
of these proceedings and to prepare a memorial    
copy for presentation to the Smith family, at     
 a time and place deemed appropriate by the        
 Sponsor.

           Resolution No. 161                      
           Skinker-DeBaliviere                     
            100th Anniversary                      
       WHEREAS, just after the 1904 World's        
 Fair, developers began to create lots for homes   
 by the fairgrounds; and                           
      WHEREAS, the first homes in the              
 Skinker-DeBaliviere neighborhood were built       
 in 1908, one hundred years ago; and               
      WHEREAS, the Skinker-DeBaliviere             
 neighborhood is a historic district; and          
        WHEREAS, Skinker DeBaliviere is the        
 first neighborhood in the City of St. Louis to    
 become       integrated     racially     and      
 socioeconomically and after concerted efforts     
 of neighbors and institutions, has become a       
 model of diversity and stability; and             
      WHEREAS, Washington University, St.          
 Roch Church, Grace United Methodist               
 Church, and Delmar Baptist Church (now            
 New Cote Brilliante Church of God) and            
 resident groups formed the Skinker                
 DeBaliviere Community Council in 1966 and         
 have supported it since; and                      
      WHEREAS, residents and the Skinker           
 DeBaliviere Community Council have                
 supported many activities to bring residents      
 together: Art Fair and House Tour, Rags to        
 Riches, Halloween Party, Christmas Walk           
 House Tour, Dinner Theatre, National Night        
 Out, Garden Tour, Community Garden, Dog           
 Show, New Neighbors Parties, and Four             
 Corners Concerts;                                 
     WHEREAS, the neighborhood has                 
 communicated through the Times of Skinker         
 DeBaliviere, St. Louis's first neighborhood       
 newspaper founded in 1970;                        
     WHEREAS, the neighborhood has been            
 home to numerous citywide elected officials       
 as well as state legislators, aldermen, and       
 committeepeople;                                  
      WHEREAS, neighborhood residents              
 continue to work to insure both diversity and     
 stability and will strive to continue their       
 success in a second hundred years                 
      NOW THEREFORE BE IT                          
 RESOLVED by the Board of Aldermen of              
 the City of St. Louis, that we wish to recognize  
 Skinker-DeBaliviere neighborhood on the           
 occasion of their One Hundredth Anniversary,      
 that we encourage them to continue in their       
 commitment to the community, and we wish          
 it many more years of success; and we further    
 direct the Clerk of this Board to spread a copy  
 of this Resolution across the minutes of this    
 proceeding and to prepare a commemorative        
 copy for presentation to our honorees at a       
 time and place deemed appropriate by the         
 sponsor.
